From soluble iodine to functional blockage in zincşiodine
flow-battery positive electrodes
Author One1 and Author Two1

Abstract The positive (iodine) electrode limits the practical current and accessible capacity of zincşiodine flow batteries (ZIFBs), yet the first appearance of oxidized iodine is not the failure event. Here we develop a multiscale positive-electrode mechanism that follows iodine from soluble oxidized species to retained native I2(s), and finally to functional blockage through a closure-conditioned accessibility loss -- separating iodine that is merely present from iodine that blocks. The organizing coordinate is a dimensionless surface super-saturation stress Seff = Sbase rZenlI2 /Brerl, built on a within-cycle baseline stress Sbase = (inv + gen)/intra: a generation/transport Damk÷hler group gen = iapp/(2F Deff csat), an inventory group inv = cI2,tot/csat and a within-cycle speciation buffer intra place current density, compression and state of charge on a common axis, while two composition factors, referred to the baseline electrolyte, carry the cross-cell chemistry -- an apparent iodine-accommodation term rBerl (supporting bromide raising the effective I2 solubility: its dominant cross-composition effect, which the buffer alone under-predicts; a  2.5Î capacity gain to an intrinsic ceiling) and an excess-iodide stress index ZrenlI2 . At the modeled baseline electrolyte Brerl = rZenlI2 = 1 and Seff = Sbase. As an internal-consistency check-- not a validation of the solver--this baseline stress reproduces the exported super-saturation field of a native continuum model, used only as a mechanistic scaffold, to R2 = 1.000 (up to a constant normalization); the two composition factors are separately calibrated to full-cell measurements (so R2 = 1.000 does not validate them), and the theory is anchored to those external operating-regime measurements and to independent dissolution kinetics. Passivation is governed by two thresholds: a precipitation onset at S = 1 and a functional-blocking onset at a closure-conditioned critical solid fraction s. The finite gap between them-- 17 mAh cm-2 for the standard cell at the half-blocking (eff = 0.5) criterion (closure-conditional, and smaller under a stricter onset)--makes the long-standing intuition "retained iodine is not blocking iodine" quantitative and falsifiable. The dissolution-limited current i = nF k (n = 2), evaluated with the independently measured rate constant k  (1.2ş2.2) Î 10-6 mol cm-2 s-1 from the dissolution kinetics of Zhao et al., sets a rate ceiling (i  232ş425 mA cm-2); the failure capacity is consistent with scaling as the accessible pore volume; and a ButlerşVolmer voltage reduction, re-anchored to the measured charge curve, fits the real pre-failure charge curve to 6.5 mV RMSE while showing that voltage alone cannot demand a coalesced film. The resulting layered framework integrates molecular priors, mesoscale closures and continuum fields into a design map: suppress the generation stress and maintain the within-cycle buffer while raising iodine accommodation; do not chase iodine amount.
1 Introduction
Zincşiodine flow batteries (ZIFBs) couple a soluble, abundant and high-potential iodide/triiodide catholyte to a zinc anode, and are attractive for low-cost stationary storage [1ş6]. Recent longlife demonstrations further underscore the importance of ion and intermediate management in this
1

chemistry [7]. In practice the positive electrode caps both the sustainable current density (typically < 100 mA cm-2) and the accessible areal capacity, through iodine precipitation and sluggish Iş /I3 ş kinetics [5, 8]. A recurring, counter-intuitive observation frames the problem: during charge the catholyte darkens with oxidized iodine well before the cell fails, so the first appearance of iodine is not the failure event. Treating "iodine is present" as "iodine has passivated the electrode" is therefore wrong, and a predictive description must separate iodine that is merely present from iodine that is functionally blocking.
The molecular origin is the poor aqueous solubility of the neutral intermediate I2. Charging proceeds as 2 I-  I2 + 2e- followed by complexation I- + I2  I3- (formation constant K  698 M-1 [4, 9]; and, in bromide-bearing electrolytes, I2 + Br-  I2Br- [3, 8]); when the dissolution/complexation step is slow, I2 super-saturates at the carbon surface and grows as a poorly conductive solid that removes active area; operando EQCM on nanoporous carbon directly resolves this solid I2 electrodeposition and the attendant pore filling [10, 11]. Zhao et al. [12] established this dissolution limitation quantitatively in a related ZnI2 flow cell, measuring a heterogeneous dissolution rate constant k  10-6 mol cm-2 s-1 by ultravioletşvisible spectroscopy and confirming, by cyclic voltammetry and chronoamperometry, that solid iodine passivates the electrode and produces an oscillating, dissolution-limited current.
A compression series makes the puzzle concrete (Fig. 1a): over-compressing the felt gives the shortest usable capacity and the earliest voltage runaway even though the model predicts it accumulates the most solid iodine. This motivates--though it does not by itself prove--the separation of retained iodine from functionally-blocking iodine. What has been missing is a quantitative mechanism framework: a compact stress coordinate, embedded in a cross-scale model stack, that (i) collapses the many device and operating variables onto a small set of dimensionless groups, (ii) distinguishes precipitation onset from functional blocking, and (iii) connects the molecular dissolution kinetics to the continuum response of a real porous electrode. Here we build that framework. We show that a single super-saturation stress reproduces the exported super-saturation field of a native continuum model, that a two-threshold criterion makes "retained = blocking" quantitative, and that the resulting failure-onset laws are consistent with our separately-reported full-cell trends and with the independently measured dissolution kinetics. The development is deliberately crossscale: density-functional and molecular-dynamics priors set the trapping and transport tendencies, mesoscale geometry fixes the accessibility and permeability closures, the continuum model integrates these in a real geometry, and the dimensionless theory distills the result into a design map.
2 Model framework and reduced iodine-state theory
Passivation is not governed by retained iodine alone, but by whether the free iodine exceeds its buffered solubility and whether the resulting solid then crosses an accessibility threshold. Our aim is to compress this picture into a single closed-form surface stress that the continuum solution obeys up to the stated normalization, and that predicts when failure occurs. We set out the physical picture and assumptions (ž2.1), state the governing balances the continuum scaffold actually solves (ž2.2), reduce the reaction front to a super-saturation stress (ž2.3), define the two failure thresholds (ž2.5), fix the dissolution-limited magnitudes (ž2.6), and record where the closures come from (ž2.8). 2.5 Two-threshold passivation: retained versus blocking
Precipitation onset does not coincide with electrode failure. We define two thresholds:
1. Precipitation onset, S = 1, reached at areal capacity Qs, where free I2 first saturates (csat being the measured aqueous I2 solubility [15]) and solid I2(s) begins to nucleate.
2. Functional blocking, s = s, reached at Qf , where the solid fraction crosses the accessibility/percolation threshold and the accessible reaction area collapses -- a non-conducting solid deposit blocking a porous electrode by percolation has precedent in porous-electrode modeling [16] and in mineral-clogging/percolation models of porous media [17].

4 Results
We present the results bottom-up because the model is built from priors and closures into a continuum state generator; the scientific question, however, is problem-driven: when does soluble or retained iodine become functionally blocking? We therefore first establish the molecular and transport bounds that fix where iodine is retained and how fast it clears (ž4.1); then use the mesoscale closures that convert retained solid I2(s) into accessibility and permeability consequences (ž4.2); and finally test whether the resulting positive-electrode state clock--through the continuum solve and the dimensionless stress that integrate and distil them--explains the observed transition from normal operation to the voltage rise, across voltage, compression and operating-knob anchors.

4.1 Molecular priors: where iodine is retained and how fast it clears
The chain begins at the molecular scale, which supplies bounds and tendencies--not rate constants-- for two quantities the upper layers inherit. Periodic CP2K places molecular I2 on the carbon surface with single-I2 adsorption energies of -0.40 (basal), -0.85 (carbonyl, C ş O) and -1.00 eV (hydroxyl, C ş OH), and -0.32 eV at a vacancy: oxygenated defects bind I2 roughly twice as strongly as pristine basal, and a two-molecule coalescence of -0.69 eV at C ş OH favours compact iodine clusters (Fig. 3a; charge-density-difference maps and projected densities of states in the SI). These set the tendency to retain and locally co-associate iodine at oxygenated sites--a placement bias, not a rate--consistent with independent DFT of iodine on carbon [24, 25]. 4.5 Dissolution-limited magnitude and independent kinetic anchor
The theory carries two quantitative magnitudes. The geometric face predicts that the low-rate failure capacity scales with the pore volume per area, Qf  L (Eq. 13, via the percolation threshold s  ); this is consistent with our full-cell compression series, reported separately, used here only as an external check on the scaling (Fig. 9a). The kinetic face predicts a rate ceiling at iapp = i0 = 2F k0: with the independently measured k0  (1.2ş2.2) Î 10-6 mol cm-2 s-1 from the in-situ iodine kinetics of Zhao et al. [12], this gives i = 232ş425 mA cm-2, the natural dissolutionlimited rate ceiling (Fig. 9b). The kinetic anchor is from an independent laboratory and electrolyte, so the magnitude scale is not fitted; it is consistent with the dissolution-limited super-saturation, not the retained amount, setting failure. Strikingly, two unrelated external anchors converge on the same ceiling: our separately-measured full-cell rate threshold (280ş320 mA cm-2) falls squarely inside the independent Zhao kinetic ceiling (232ş425 mA cm-2) -- a coincidence the dissolutionlimited picture predicts, but to which neither anchor was tuned.

4.7 Bulk permeability loss is small within the smooth network closure

Injecting the physically derived pore-network permeability K(s) in place of the mean-field Kozenyş

Carman closure and re-solving the continuum model shifts the cell voltage by only 1.27 mV (Supple-

mentary Fig. S6); the relative permeability stays near unity throughout (KozenyşCarman endpoint

0.96, pore-network endpoint 0.98). Likewise the internal overpotential decomposition shows that

the late voltage response is carried by the electronic/contact and accessibility terms, not by bulk

hydraulic clogging. Failure is loss of accessible reaction sites, consistent with a slow pore/interface

impedance state in the pre-failure regime (not solution resistance; the noisy failure-regime spectra

are not interpreted, SI) and with the dissolution-limited mechanism. This bounds only the homoge-

blockage, collector-side shell formation, or post-onset hydraulic/morphological failure in real felt

under heterogeneous compression.

5 Mechanistic analysis of operating and design knobs
The operating and design variables examined here act on positive-electrode iodine passivation through the dimensionless stress of Eqs. (10)ş(11), baseline Sbase = (inv+gen)/intra (Sec. 2), with gen = iapp/(2F Deff csat), inv = cI2,tot/csat,  = 1+KIcI- +KBrcBr and the Bruggeman diffusivity Deff = D01.5. This projects the tested fixed-csat scan families onto a single phase plane (Fig. 11): each knob is a distinct move in the (gen, inv) plane relative to one precipitation-onset boundary. Because the buffer at onset is nearly constant across cells (  760), that boundary is an almost fixed budget line, gen + inv  253, and the fixed-csat scan families fall on this budget line within the solved branch: rate, diffusivity and compression slide the operating point along gen, while state of charge climbs it vertically in inv until it crosses -- the buffer falling from  1263 to 554 along this charge trajectory is what drives S upward. Two knobs instead move the boundary: salting-out, which rescales csat and shifts the whole plane, and -- weakly in this range -- supporting bromide, which raises  and nudges the budget outward. Conductivity and flow move the point not at all: they are the negative control made geometric. Precipitation begins when the point crosses the onset boundary (S = 1, onset capacity QS=1); functional blocking occurs a finite distance further out, at eff = 0.5 (blocking capacity Q=0.5), and the strip between the two boundaries is the quantitative "retained = blocking" reservoir. The accessible fraction is Abare = 1 - eff , the dissolution-limited current ceiling is i = 2F k [12], and the failure capacity scales with the precipitate reservoir L. Free-I2 transport (diffusivity prior)
Free-I2 diffusivity is the single most leveraged knob in the model, and the reason is structural: it enters S only through the generation term, gen  1/Deff  1/D0, leaving inv and  untouched. Physically, gen is the diffusion-polarization overshoot -- the excess free-I2 concentration that must accumulate at the pore wall to drive the electrogenerated flux iapp/2F outward through the Nernst layer .
Halving the species diffusivity therefore doubles the required local pore-wall overshoot, so the surface concentration overtakes csat far sooner: precipitation onset advances from the baseline QS1 = 83.1 to 45.1 (the volume-averaged peak stress is nearly unchanged, Savg,peak = 1.78 vs. 1.76 baseline -- it is the onset shift, not the averaged peak, that registers the doubled local overshoot, the same averaging caveat that applies to the rate and wetted-area knobs below), the solid inventory grows 52Î (s,end : 3.22 Î 10-3  1.67 Î 10-1), blocking saturates (end  1.000, Abare,end  2 Î 10-8), pore permeability falls 74 % (Kperm,end : 0.956  0.252), and the accessibility loss lifts the cell voltage (Vend : 1.729  1.849 V).

In the ranked sensitivity the diffusion prior is first of nine families (effecttotal = 20.24; dominant node solidinventory), roughly twice the next knob and five times current density. Because gen is the largest mobile term in S at the operating current and scales as 1/D, transport sets both the precipitation onset and the post-onset blocking severity simultaneously -- making it the master lever on the dissolution-limited supersaturation pathway.
The principal caveats are that the scan resolves only the two ▒2Î points at fixed Q = 120, so the continuous S(D) law is inferred rather than mapped; that Deff folds in the l1.5 factor so a porosity change would compound this sensitivity; and that the accompanying voltage swing is a consequence of accessibility loss, not an independent transport effect. The diffusivity prior is correspondingly the dominant source of parametric uncertainty in any absolute lifetime estimate, so the load-bearing conclusion is the ranking, not a calibrated D0.

Mechanical compression
Compression enters the theory through two distinct, opposing terms while leaving the chemical thresholds untouched. Compressing the felt simultaneously thins it (L : 3.0  1.5 mm) and lowers the porosity (l : 0.900  0.800). Through the Bruggeman relation Deff = D0l1.5 a lower porosity throttles intra-pore transport and so raises the generation stress gen  1/1l .5 (through Deff only; L enters not here but the separate pore-volume capacity below); this transport channel is, however, weak and not cleanly monotonic, because the 1.5 exponent over the realistic porosity window changes Deff by only 0.93 to 1.11Î while L co-varies in the same direction. The simulated peak stress is consequently flat: Savg,peak spans only 1.7214 (L=3.0) to 1.7590 (baseline L=2.0), with the tightest felt (L=1.5) sitting marginally below baseline at 1.7570 -- a < 2% non-monotonic excursion.
The decisive channel is geometric: compression shrinks the accessible pore volume L (computed from the solve's own (L, ) pairs, L relative to the L=2.0 baseline is 0.71/1.29/1.59Î for L=1.5/2.5/3.0 mm), the reservoir the precipitate must fill before it occludes the pore. Packing the same precipitated mass into a smaller volume drives the occluded fraction toward unity sooner: at Q = 120 the tight felt reaches end = 0.9998 with negligible retained area (Abare,end = 1.95 Î 10-4), whereas the loose felt holds end = 0.9204 and Abare,end = 7.93 Î 10-2 -- a  407Î difference in surviving accessibility, and unlike the stress channel this trend is strictly monotonic. Equivalently, the blocking onset rises monotonically Q=0.5 : 97.6  103.3 mA h cm-2 (+5.9%) as the felt is relaxed, while the precipitation onset shifts only marginally (QS=1 : 82.4  84.3).

Compression is therefore not used here as a one-parameter proof of pore-volume control; its mechanistic value is that it separates iodine retention, blocking efficiency and voltage/contact compensation in a real electrode geometry, reinforcing that retained iodine is not blocking iodine (Discussion).
Active-material inventory
Charged iodine inventory (state of charge) is the state variable whose accumulation drives the onset, because it sets the absolute quantity of free I2 that the finite-volume, saturation-limited pore liquid must accommodate, entering S through the inventory group inv = cI2,tot/csat. With csat fixed and gen held constant across the sweep, charging raises cI2,tot so that inv  Q/Qbase (a stateof-charge ramp on the baseline configuration, not an independent iodide molarity variation -- see the caveat below). The native capacity-window scan makes this quantitative and reveals a sharply super-linear, threshold-type response: the precipitated solid fraction climbs from s,end = 9.0 Î 10-8 at Q = 80 mA h cm-2 to 1.25 Î 10-4 at 100 and 3.22 Î 10-3 at 120 mA h cm-2 -- a 25.8Î jump in solid for only a 20 % inventory increase between 100 and 120 mA h cm-2, and 3.6 Î 104Î across the full window -- because near S = 1 a modest inventory increment converts a large excess of supersaturated free I2 into I2(s).
The dimensionless theory localizes the two operative thresholds: precipitation onset at QS=1 = 83.1 mA h cm-2 (independently recovered as the baseline QS1 = 83.11) and functional blocking at Q=0.5 = 99.67 mA h cm-2, in quantitative agreement with the simulated accessibility, whose blocked fraction crosses eff = 0.5 right at 100 mA h cm-2 (end : 0.335  0.519  0.824 over Q = 96  100  108) and saturates at 0.990 by 120 mA h cm-2. The accessibility collapse, not any conductivity change, drives the end-of-life overpotential (Vend : 1.427  1.454  1.601  1.729 V over Q = 80  100  115  120).
That the solid-inventory node dominates failure is corroborated across the ensemble: the two largest-effect families both report solid-inventory as their dominant node (node weights 16.68 and 6.49), but they reach it through different terms of S -- the diffusion prior (20.24) through Deff in gen, and the salting-out activity (9.55) through csat in inv -- confirming that whether one throttles removal or shrinks the solubility headroom, the failure is registered as solid accumulation.

Salting-out (apparent solubility)
The saturation concentration csat is not a fixed material constant but an apparent solubility set by the supporting electrolyte: the concentrated ZnI2/NH4Br medium salts I2 out of solution, lowering csat and shrinking the solubility headroom. Because csat enters S both as the denominator of the inventory group inv = cI2,tot/csat and (through Deff csat) of the generation group, a lower csat raises the stress on both channels and brings S = 1 forward. In the cross-knob ensemble this makes saltingout the second-ranked family overall (effect 9.55, behind only the free-I2 diffusivity prior at 20.24), and like the inventory knob it registers its impact predominantly at the solid-inventory node (node weight 6.49). The lever is therefore two-sided: additives that salt I2 in -- cosolvents or complexing agents that raise the apparent csat -- are a direct route to keeping S < 1, the chemical counterpart to raising the speciation buffer . We treat csat as a bounded constant here (1.33 mol m-3); it is the most consequential untested parameter in the theory (Discussion, bound iv).
Supporting bromide
Supporting bromide enters the theory exclusively through the complexation buffer in the denominator of S,  = 1 + KIcI- + KBrcBr-, so that S/cBr = -(inv + gen) KBr/2 < 0. Crucially NH4Br perturbs neither numerator driver -- not gen and not inv -- so it acts purely as a speciation buffer, complexing dissolved I2/I-3 and dividing down the free-I2 supersaturation. In the native scan (2 M, 3 M, 5 M) the buffer rises only modestly, end = 543.2  561.5 (+3.4%), and the stress response is correspondingly weak: peak stress falls from Savg,peak = 1.772 to 1.743 ( 1.6%), shifting precipitation onset QS=1 : 81.2  84.0 and blocking Q=0.5 : 97.6  100.7 -- gains of only  3 capacity units ( 3%).
The one appreciable signature is the precipitated inventory, which drops  3.9Î (s,end : 7.47 Î 10-3  1.90 Î 10-3) with a small recovery of accessible carbon (Abare,end : 5.0 Î 10-3  1.5 Î 10-2); accordingly the ranking assigns bromide a dominant node of solid-inventory but an effecttotal of just 0.71 -- sixth of nine families and roughly 28Î weaker than the diffusion prior. The physical reading is instructive: because failure is governed by dissolution-limited free-I2 generation against a fixed solubility ceiling, a buffer already near   550 has almost no marginal leverage -- bromide can shave the supersaturation peak and thin the I2(s) film, but it cannot remove the I2 being produced, so eff remains pinned near unity once onset is passed.
This modeled weakness carries an explicit caveat: the static, lumped  closure (constant KBr, fixed csat and dissolution constant k) cannot represent concentration-dependent polybromide/polyiodide equilibria or the genuine rise in effective I2 solubility in bromide-rich electrolytes, so the simulation almost certainly under-predicts the true protective magnitude of NH4Br. The model is therefore reliable for the direction and mechanism (-buffering of S, reduced s) [29ş31] but not for the quantitative size of the bromide benefit.

The electrode specific (wetted) area as enters S heuristically through the generation group, since the locally relevant flux is the area-specific current density ilocal = iapp/(asL) (with as the volumetric area, m-1), so that more wetted area dilutes the local generation and, to first order, gen  1/as while inv,  and the pore-volume capacity L are left untouched; this is a local-flux interpretation, as as does not appear in the planar-Nernst-layer gen of Eq. Non-drivers: conductivity and flow (a mechanistic contrast)
Liquid conductivity (l), carbon-felt electronic conductivity (s), and electrolyte flow rate (Qflow) form a distinct class of voltage-channel parameters that, by construction, cannot appear in S. The conductivities enter only the charge-transport law (i = -ll, i = -ss) and therefore set the ohmic overpotential and cell voltage, while Qflow touches only the external boundary layer; none alters the per-coulomb I2 generation, csat, , Deff , or lL. The scan demonstrates this decoupling quantitatively at fixed galvanostatic Q = 120: varying l over 10 S m-1 to 40 S m-1 swings Vend by 77 mV yet leaves the onset unchanged at QS1 = 83.1 (spread 0.00, end = 3 Î 10-4); s over 80 S m-1 to 320 S m-1 produces the largest ohmic excursion in the whole study (141 mV) but again pins the onset at 83.1; and Qflow (25 mL min-1 to 100 mL min-1) moves Vend by only 8 mV and QS1 by 0.22.
The ranking places these at the bottom of the spectrum -- l is the sole parameter whose dominant node is voltage (effecttotal = 0.12), with Qflow (0.20) and s (0.84) close behind -- one to two orders of magnitude below the true drivers. The single instructive nuance is s: a poorly conducting felt redistributes the reaction toward the membrane side, locally raising the I2 flux and thus the end-state precipitated inventory (s,end = 7.2Î10-3 at 80 S m-1 vs. 1.6Î10-3 at 320 S m-1), but this is post-onset current crowding rather than a shift of the supersaturation threshold, which is precisely why QS1 does not move.
The mechanistic conclusion is itself a result: iodine passivation lives in the stress channel (S) whereas these parameters act on the orthogonal voltage channel (Ohm's law), so ohmic engineering buys round-trip efficiency but cannot cure the dissolution-limited supersaturation failure -- a clean negative control that sharpens identification of the genuine drivers. (Viscosity, conceptually a member of this class via Qflow/D, is omitted from the numerical claim because its scan rows failed to converge.)
Design corollary
Read through Eq. (11), the knob ensemble yields a single, self-consistent design rule. Because failure is the crossing of S = 1 followed by the growth of eff to blocking, mitigation must (i) lower the

External consistency
The model trends above were checked against our full-cell measurements, which are reported in full in a separate experimental study; a representative operating-regime subset is tabulated in Supplementary Table S3, and here these data serve only as an external-consistency anchor, not as a validation of the model. Any short potential-step (CA/EIS) perturbation is likewise treated only as a low-dose, short-time accessibility check; it is not used to claim that early iodine is intrinsically non-passivating, and it does not rule out passivation after larger retained-iodine inventory, longer exposure, or morphology/connectivity changes -- the regime the continuum model assigns to the high-capacity blocking threshold.

6 Discussion
The theory reorganizes a large body of device phenomenology around one dimensionless stress and two thresholds. Three consequences follow. First, "retained = blocking" is no longer a slogan but a measurable gap Q between S = 1 and s; the design objective is to widen it. The same supersaturationşprecipitationşaccessibility-loss logic recurs in other aqueous battery electrodes -- local supersaturation precipitates a solid that occludes the active surface, as established for ZnO films on zinc anodes and for solubility-controlled Li2S passivation of sulfur cathodes [32, 33] -- so the framework is not iodine-specific. Made quantitative (Fig. 14), this universality is itself a design principle: the inventory stress inv = cI2,tot/csat scales inversely with the solubility headroom csat of the condensable intermediate, so iodine is structurally the most exposed chemistry. Second, the tested levers act on different nodes of the stress framework rather than a single

This study isolates the positive-electrode precipitationşpassivation channel; it is complementary to
the iodine crossover/shuttle and membrane losses addressed elsewhere [7, 31], which act on inter-
electrode transport rather than on the surface super-saturation stress S. The picture is bounded, and
we state the bounds precisely. (i) The continuum model is a mechanism scaffold, not an exact voltage
validator (absolute Vend is over-blocked by  0.24 V). (ii) The two-threshold capacities are computed under the empirical accessibility closure; swapping it for the physical mesoscale closure preserves
the ordering but changes the absolute blocking magnitude, so Qf and the Q gap are closureconditional. (iii) The functional-blocking solid fraction s is calibrated, and its fiber-model crosscheck shares the same accessibility mapping, so the agreement is consistency, not full independence. 7 Conclusion
We have shown that positive-electrode passivation in zincşiodine flow batteries is a dissolutionlimited super-saturation phenomenon governed by a single dimensionless stress Seff (baseline Sbase = (inv + gen)/intra) and a two-threshold (precipitation, functional blocking) criterion. The theory reproduces the model's exported super-saturation field as an internal-consistency check, orders the tested operating and design knobs, recovers the failure-capacity and rate-threshold scalings, and is anchored to independently measured dissolution kinetics. Crucially, the pre-failure voltage rise is dominated by iodide-depletion concentration overpotential, with accessibility loss entering only late, so the absolute voltage cannot by itself demand a coalesced passivating film--the blocking morphology must instead be inferred from the mesoscale geometry and percolation. It thus separates iodine that is present from iodine that blocks from iodine that exacts a voltage penalty, and it prescribes the design map--suppress generation stress, raise iodine accommodation (keep the withincycle buffer), do not chase iodine amount.
