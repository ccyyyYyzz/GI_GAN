# Round 48 — The one remaining parameter-free transform after positive FOHI

## Decision

**TEST ONE NEW TRANSFORM.**

FOHI is now the frozen incumbent: at cutoff 0.12, transition 0.03, and alpha 0.50, all three independent 512-image validation pairings improve PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS over their matched structural arms with entirely favorable paired confidence intervals. The removed parallel energy is also decisively non-negligible at 12.4%–18.1%.

There is nevertheless one more fundamental zero-training operation that must be tested before the method can carry its cleanest causal interpretation. It is not a learned gate, a new spectral design, a tangent-cone module, or a retuned coefficient. It is to **quotient both reconstruction arms by the exact box-fiber projection before defining either the structural axis or the GAN innovation**.

I call the resulting one-shot diagnostic **endpoint-quotiented FOHI (EQ-FOHI)**. Its purpose is not cosmetic. The current code evaluates the structural reference as the exact projection of the clamped control prediction, while it constructs FOHI from the raw null correction and anchors the final proposal at `base + raw_control_correction`. Consequently, setting alpha to zero in the current FOHI path need not reproduce the structural reference. Active box faces make those two projections genuinely different in general.

EQ-FOHI removes that non-nested baseline and all proposal components that are invisible after exact box-fiber projection. It is the only additional parameter-free transform I recommend testing. Do not test a tangent-cone projection in parallel, do not restore norms, and do not retune any frozen value.

The held-out test remains closed. The running 2%/10% measurement-rate campaign should not be interrupted.

## 1. Geometry of the remaining issue

Let

$$
\mathcal C_y=\mathcal F_y=\{x\in[0,1]^n:Ax=y\}
                =\{x\in[0,1]^n:Qx=z\},
$$

where the rows of $Q$ are an orthonormal basis for the numerical row space of $A$. Write

$$
P_R=Q^\top Q,\qquad P_N=I-P_R.
$$

The stable base $x_B$ is already in $\mathcal C_y$. The structural and adversarial networks produce internal proposals. In the current implementation, `predict_all` returns both

1. a clamped image proposal, and
2. a raw correction that has been projected into $\ker A$ before the clamp.

The structural reference used in the Round 47 evidence is

$$
x_S=\Pi_{\mathcal C_y}(p_S),
$$

where $p_S$ is the clamped structural prediction. By contrast, the current FOHI proposal is assembled from the raw correction $c_S^{\rm raw}$ as

$$
x_B+c_S^{\rm raw}+\alpha v_{\rm raw},
$$

and only then projected exactly. Therefore its zero-innovation endpoint is

$$
\Pi_{\mathcal C_y}(x_B+c_S^{\rm raw}),
$$

which is not algebraically required to equal $x_S=\Pi_{\mathcal C_y}(\operatorname{clip}(x_B+c_S^{\rm raw}))$.

For projection onto the box alone the two coincide. For projection onto the intersection of the box and a measurement affine space, they need not. Clipping changes the row-space record, and the subsequent exact projection can redistribute the correction across active and free pixels.

This is the final geometric confound left after Round 45: FOHI removed structural rescaling in the ambient null space, but it has not yet quotiented out the normal directions created by active box faces.

## 2. The endpoint quotient is unavoidable

Define the exact endpoint map

$$
R_y(p)=\Pi_{\mathcal C_y}(p).
$$

For any nonempty closed convex set $\mathcal C$ and its normal cone

$$
N_{\mathcal C}(x)=\{n:\langle n,w-x\rangle\le 0\ \text{for every }w\in\mathcal C\},
$$

the projection optimality condition gives

$$
\boxed{R(p)=x\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad p-x\in N_{\mathcal C}(x).}
$$

Thus all proposals in

$$
x+N_{\mathcal C}(x)
$$

have the same exact feasible endpoint. They differ only by a projection-normal component.

For the box-constrained measurement fiber, the normal cone is generated by the measurement row space and the active box normals:

$$
N_{\mathcal C_y}(x)
=
\operatorname{range}(Q^\top)+N_{[0,1]^n}(x),
$$

where a box-normal coordinate is nonpositive at a lower face $x_i=0$, zero in the interior, and nonnegative at an upper face $x_i=1$.

The existing null projection $P_N$ removes a row-space normal. It does not remove the null-space component of an active-box normal. Therefore two raw proposals with the same exact reconstruction can still produce different filtered and orthogonalized raw innovations.

This motivates a necessary invariance principle.

### Principle — endpoint or normal-gauge invariance

A fusion rule claiming to combine the final structural and GAN reconstructions on the same physical measurement fiber should be unchanged when either internal proposal is replaced by another proposal with the same exact box-fiber endpoint.

The current raw-correction FOHI is generally not invariant under that replacement. An endpoint-based rule is invariant by construction.

## 3. The single proposed transform: EQ-FOHI

Use the already frozen network outputs and exact projector. Let

$$
x_S=R_y(p_S),\qquad x_G=R_y(p_G),
$$

where $p_S$ and $p_G$ are the existing clamped structural and GAN predictions, respectively. No network or checkpoint changes.

Define the exact feasible chords

$$
s=x_S-x_B,
\qquad
 d=x_G-x_S.
$$

Because all three endpoints are in the same measurement fiber,

$$
s,d\in\ker A.
$$

Moreover, convexity gives the finite feasible-segment certificate

$$
x_S+t d=(1-t)x_S+t x_G\in\mathcal C_y,
\qquad 0\le t\le 1.
$$

Thus $d$ is not merely a null direction. It is a physically admissible chord and lies in the radial cone, hence the tangent cone, of $\mathcal C_y$ at $x_S$.

Keep the frozen smooth high-pass operator and its null-space compression:

$$
K=P_NH_{0.12,0.03}P_N.
$$

Form

$$
u=Kd.
$$

Remove only the component parallel to the exact structural chord:

$$
v=
\begin{cases}
 u-\dfrac{\langle u,s\rangle}{\|s\|_2^2}s,&\|s\|_2>0,\\[6pt]
 u,&\|s\|_2=0.
\end{cases}
$$

The frozen reconstruction is

$$
\boxed{
\widehat x_{\rm EQ}
=
\Pi_{\mathcal C_y}\left(x_S+0.50\,v\right).
}
$$

This is one transform: replace raw proposal coordinates by exact box-fiber endpoint chords, then apply the already frozen FOHI operation. There is no norm restoration, no learned coefficient, no new threshold, and no truth at inference.

## 4. Theorem package

### Proposition 1 — endpoint invariance

EQ-FOHI depends only on $(x_B,x_S,x_G)$ and is therefore invariant to every replacement

$$
p_S\mapsto p'_S,\qquad p_G\mapsto p'_G
$$

satisfying

$$
R_y(p'_S)=R_y(p_S),\qquad R_y(p'_G)=R_y(p_G).
$$

Equivalently, it is invariant to arbitrary changes of the proposal-normal components inside the corresponding normal cones.

**Proof.** The construction uses the internal proposals only through $x_S=R_y(p_S)$ and $x_G=R_y(p_G)$. If those endpoints are unchanged, then $s$, $d$, $u$, $v$, and the final proposal are unchanged. ∎

This is the substantive improvement over raw FOHI. It turns the method into a function of exact feasible reconstructions, rather than a function of arbitrary preprojection representatives.

### Proposition 2 — unique nearest endpoint innovation

Let

$$
\mathcal T_s=\ker A\cap s^\perp.
$$

Then the EQ-FOHI direction is the unique solution of

$$
\boxed{
 v=\arg\min_{w\in\mathcal T_s}\|w-Kd\|_2^2.
}
$$

**Proof.** Since $d\in\ker A$ and $K=P_NHP_N$, $u=Kd\in\ker A$. Also $s\in\ker A$. For $s\ne0$, the orthogonal projector from $\ker A$ onto $\ker A\cap s^\perp$ subtracts the rank-one component $\langle u,s\rangle s/\|s\|^2$. Orthogonal projection onto a closed subspace is the unique Euclidean nearest point. For $s=0$, the constraint $s^\perp$ is vacuous and $v=u$. ∎

A useful axiomatic form follows. Once the frozen filter $K$ and exact endpoints are fixed, EQ-FOHI is the unique rule satisfying all four conditions:

1. endpoint invariance;
2. $v\in\ker A$;
3. no change of the exact structural coefficient, $\langle v,s\rangle=0$;
4. minimum Euclidean alteration of the filtered feasible chord $Kd$.

Any different rule must abandon at least one of those conditions or introduce another geometry.

### Proposition 3 — exact nesting and exact measurement consistency

For every scalar $\alpha$,

$$
A(x_S+\alpha v)=y.
$$

In particular,

$$
\widehat x_{\rm EQ}(0)=x_S.
$$

For the frozen coefficient,

$$
\widehat x_{\rm EQ}=\Pi_{\mathcal C_y}(x_S+0.5v)\in\mathcal C_y.
$$

Therefore the method is exactly nested in the matched structural arm and exactly consistent with the displayed GI record after the certified projection.

For every truth $x^\star\in\mathcal C_y$, the Euclidean projection inequality gives

$$
\|\widehat x_{\rm EQ}-x^\star\|_2^2
+
\|x_S+0.5v-\widehat x_{\rm EQ}\|_2^2
\le
\|x_S+0.5v-x^\star\|_2^2.
$$

The final projector therefore cannot worsen squared error relative to its own preprojection proposal.

### Proposition 4 — exact distortion-improvement interval

For any nonzero endpoint innovation $v$, define

$$
a=\langle x^\star-x_S,v\rangle,
\qquad
b=\|v\|_2^2.
$$

Before the final box projection,

$$
\frac1n\|x_S+\alpha v-x^\star\|_2^2
-
\frac1n\|x_S-x^\star\|_2^2
=
\frac{\alpha^2b-2\alpha a}{n}.
$$

Hence a positive coefficient strictly improves MSE exactly when

$$
a>0,
\qquad
0<\alpha<\frac{2a}{b}.
$$

The line optimum is $\alpha^\star=a/b$. If the frozen proposal satisfies this interval, the final exact projection preserves the strict improvement by Proposition 3. Projection may also improve samples outside the preprojection interval, but that is not guaranteed.

In population form, for a direction source $q$,

$$
A_q=\mathbb E\langle x^\star-x_S,v_q\rangle,
\qquad
B_q=\mathbb E\|v_q\|_2^2,
$$

and

$$
R_q(\alpha)-R_S
=
\frac{\alpha^2B_q-2\alpha A_q}{n}
$$

before final projection. A generic diverse direction with $A_q=0$ pays a strictly positive energy penalty. Diversity is not sufficient; positive alignment is the causal quantity.

### Proposition 5 — precise meaning of adversarial alignment

Apply the identical endpoint quotient, filter, orthogonalization, coefficient, and exact projection to every proposal source. Let $v_G$ be the adversarial direction, $v_0$ the discriminator-off direction, and $v_2$ the second-VQAE direction.

The statement “the gain is adversarial alignment” means that the adversarial training changes the distribution of the feasible endpoint chord so that $v_G$ enters the joint descent cone of pixel and perceptual losses more reliably than $v_0$ and $v_2$.

For differentiable losses $\ell_j$ and directional smoothness constants $L_j$, a sufficient local condition is

$$
g_{j,q}=\langle\nabla_x\ell_j(x_S,x^\star),v_q\rangle<0
$$

for distortion, perceptual loss, and $1-\mathrm{SSIM}$, together with

$$
0<\alpha<
\min_j
\frac{-2g_{j,q}}{L_j\|v_q\|_2^2}.
$$

This is not guaranteed by high frequency or by a GAN label. It must be established by matched controls after the common deterministic geometry has been fixed.

## 5. Why no tangent-cone or optical-metric transform should be tested now

### 5.1 Tangent-cone projection is not the next transform

At the exact structural endpoint,

$$
T_{\mathcal C_y}(x_S)
=
\{h\in\ker A:
 h_i\ge0\text{ if }(x_S)_i=0,
 h_i\le0\text{ if }(x_S)_i=1\}.
$$

One could project $Kd$ onto $T_{\mathcal C_y}(x_S)\cap s^\perp$. That projection is parameter-free and has a nearest-cone property. It is nevertheless not the right next experiment.

First, $d=x_G-x_S$ is already a finite feasible chord before filtering. Second, the existing final operation $\Pi_{\mathcal C_y}(x_S+0.5v)$ is the exact finite-step box-fiber projection. Tangent-cone projection is only the first-order directional derivative of that operation near zero step. At the finite frozen coefficient 0.50, active faces can release and new faces can activate. A local cone projection can discard useful inward motion and then the final projector is still required.

It would therefore add a second per-image constrained optimization without a theorem of truth-risk dominance. Testing it together with endpoint quotient would also destroy the one-change falsification. Do not add it in Round 48.

### 5.2 The GI operator supplies no nondegenerate null-space metric

Any measurement-energy metric of the form

$$
\|h\|_W^2=h^\top A^\top WAh
$$

vanishes for every $h\in\ker A$. Singular-value weighting has the same degeneracy. Thus the optics cannot canonically rank two directions after they have been restricted to the exact measurement null space. A nonzero covariance, Fisher, perceptual, or learned weighting would come from an additional prior and would introduce choices beyond the frozen method.

The only unavoidable geometry not already used by FOHI is the box-fiber endpoint quotient. That is why EQ-FOHI is the sole new transform worth one test.

## 8. What is genuinely novel and what is standard

The theorem capable of carrying a journal-level methodological claim is the **box-fiber normal-gauge/endpoint-quotient theorem combined with the unique structural-orthogonal filtered chord**:

> Exact projection identifies an equivalence class of network proposals differing by measurement and active-box normal components. Quotienting structural and adversarial arms to their exact GI box-fiber endpoints, then taking the unique nearest high-pass null direction orthogonal to the exact structural chord, yields an endpoint-invariant and exactly nested fusion. Matched source controls then test whether adversarial training changes alignment inside the joint pixel/perceptual descent cone.

The underlying convex projection characterization, Pythagorean inequality, orthogonal-projector formula, tangent-cone formula, and one-dimensional quadratic risk interval are standard mathematical ingredients. They should not individually be advertised as new theorems.

The potentially new contribution is their combination into a causal design principle for GAN-assisted GI:

- proposals are quotiented by exact physical feasibility;
- the structural arm is an exact nested baseline;
- the GAN contribution is a feasible-endpoint chord innovation rather than an off-box internal correction;
- the only retained innovation is the unique nearest null component that does not rescale the structural coordinate;
- causal controls act after the identical quotient and geometry.

Do not claim that the GAN measures missing photons, that endpoint projection proves semantic truth, that active box faces make the inverse unique, that tangent-cone projection is necessary, that an optical metric exists on $\ker A$, that triple dominance is universal, or that any held-out result exists.
