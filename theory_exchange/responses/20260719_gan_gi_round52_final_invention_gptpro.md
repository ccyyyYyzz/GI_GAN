# Round 52 — Final invention and pre-test freeze: VQGAN-guided fiber-orthogonal high-pass innovation

## Final decision

\[
\boxed{\textbf{FREEZE FOHI.  ADD NO FURTHER TRANSFORM.}}
\]

The final journal method is **VQGAN-guided fiber-orthogonal high-pass innovation (FOHI)**.  The qualifier “VQGAN-guided” is essential: the selected residual adapter has adversarial weight zero, but its proposal direction is generated by a pretrained VQGAN prior whose codebook and decoder were shaped by adversarial and feature-matching training.

**One-sentence contribution.**  FOHI isolates the high-frequency-dominant part of a pretrained VQGAN proposal that lies in the unmeasured ghost-imaging fiber and is orthogonal to the distortion-favorable structural correction, then projects the result exactly onto the box-constrained measurement fiber, improving PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS without an adversarial fusion loss or an inference-time gate.

The frozen inference rule is

\[
d=c_G-c_S,\qquad
u=P_NH_{0.12,0.03}P_Nd,
\]

\[
v=
\begin{cases}
u-\dfrac{\langle u,c_S\rangle}{\|c_S\|_2^2}c_S,
&\|c_S\|_2>0,\\[6pt]
u,&\|c_S\|_2=0,
\end{cases}
\]

\[
\boxed{
\widehat x_{\rm FOHI}
=
\Pi_{\mathcal F_y}
\left(x_B+c_S+0.50\,v\right).
}
\]

The selected proposal arm is exactly cell B from the four-cell campaign:

- proposal source: pretrained VQGAN reconstruction;
- residual adapter source arm: `gan`;
- adapter adversarial weight: `0`;
- adapter rotation scale: `0.5`;
- adapter LPIPS weight: `0.003`;
- high-pass cutoff: `0.12`;
- transition: `0.03`;
- innovation coefficient: `0.50`;
- final operation: certified Euclidean projection onto the exact box-constrained measurement fiber.

There is no norm restoration, learned spectral gate, per-image coefficient, endpoint quotient, tangent-cone stage, low-pass addition, or post-test parameter selection.

## 1. Why the evidence closes the invention search

The completed falsification chain is unusually decisive because every candidate was forced through the same deterministic measurement geometry.

| Evidence | Result | Architectural consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Three-seed 5% FOHI freeze | 3/3 pairings jointly improve PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS; 12.4%–18.1% of filtered energy removed as structural-parallel | Rank-one orthogonalization is active and reproducible, not a cosmetic rewrite |
| Endpoint quotient | Tiny PSNR/SSIM gain, but direct LPIPS interval against FOHI crosses zero; alpha-zero anchor shift is only about \(10^{-6}\) dB | Kill EQ-FOHI; active-box representative ambiguity is not a material source of the gain |
| A versus B | No significant advantage from adding an adversarial loss to the small adapter | Remove the adapter discriminator |
| VQGAN source versus second VQAE source | VQGAN-source arms are 3/3 positive; second-VQAE arm is 0/3 because LPIPS worsens in every pairing | The causal object is the VQGAN proposal geometry, not generic network diversity |
| High-pass versus low-pass | Both can help, but direct high-pass-minus-low-pass intervals favor high-pass in all three metrics | High frequency is the dominant comparative-advantage band, not the exclusive beneficial band |
| 10%, 5%, 2% rates | 10%: 3/3; 5%: 3/3; 2%: 2/3 with one entirely unfavorable LPIPS interval | Declare 5%–10% as the validated operating envelope; 2% is outside the robust perceptual regime |
| Three independent 5% operators | 3/3 distinct matrix hashes pass; hierarchical intervals are favorable in all three metrics | Permit a narrow operator-seed-robust simulation claim within the tested structured operator family |

No remaining transformation is both structurally forced and untested:

1. **Endpoint quotient has already failed its direct replacement rule.**
2. **A tangent-cone projection is not forced.**  The exact final convex projection already handles active box faces at the finite step actually used for reconstruction.
3. **Norm restoration is anti-minimal.**  It changes the magnitude after the nearest-subspace projection and therefore abandons the minimum-change property.
4. **Adding the low-pass complement is not forced.**  It is equivalent to introducing a second band coefficient or reverting toward an unfiltered displacement; either choice requires a new selection rule.
5. **A learned gate or band search adds capacity and a new validation degree of freedom.**
6. **An “optical-energy” reweighting cannot be intrinsic on the exact null space.**  For every \(w\in\ker A\),
   \[
   \|Aw\|_2^2=w^\top A^\top A w=0.
   \]
   The measurement metric is therefore degenerate on precisely the directions being fused.  A positive-definite metric on \(\ker A\) must come from an extra prior, covariance model, or regularizer; it is not supplied by the GI record and would no longer be parameter-free physics.

FOHI is therefore final, conditional on the explicit Euclidean image geometry already used by the exact box-fiber projector.  This is a conditional uniqueness claim, not a claim that no conceivable prior-dependent fusion rule can exist.

## 2. Optics-first derivation

### 2.1 What the bucket measurements determine

For an object \(x^\star\in[0,1]^n\), displayed patterns \(a_j\), and ideal bucket measurements,

\[
y_j=\langle a_j,x^\star\rangle,\qquad y=Ax^\star.
\]

Let \(Q\in\mathbb R^{r\times n}\) have orthonormal rows spanning the numerical row space of \(A\).  The intrinsic record is \(z=Qx^\star\), and

\[
P_R=Q^\top Q,\qquad P_N=I-P_R.
\]

The ideal measurement identifies \(P_Rx^\star\), but it cannot distinguish any two objects whose difference lies in \(\ker A=\ker Q\).  With the physical intensity box, the admissible set is the compact convex measurement fiber

\[
\mathcal F_y
=
\{x\in[0,1]^n:Ax=y\}
=
\{x\in[0,1]^n:Qx=z\}.
\]

At the 5% configuration, \(n=4096\) and the numerical rank is 200, leaving 3896 affine degrees of freedom before active box faces are imposed.  The box can reduce local freedom, but it does not supply semantic information about which texture or edge arrangement is correct.

The operator family itself explains part of the observed spectral result.  It deliberately includes a DC row, low-frequency DCT rows, low-sequency Hadamard rows, and zero-mean random rows.  Coarse intensity and broad structure are consequently represented more directly than many fine spatial modes.  A stable structural method is therefore already strong on the modes most directly supported by the optics; the largest remaining comparative advantage of a natural-image adversarial prior is expected in edge and texture directions.  Because the measured row space is not identical to an ideal Fourier low-pass subspace, and because box projection couples pixels, useful low-frequency null components can remain.  The evidence therefore supports **high-pass dominance**, not high-pass exclusivity.

### 2.2 VQAE and VQGAN choose different coordinates on the same fiber

The cache construction first forms a measurement-conditioned anchor \(x_0\), then decodes two frozen vector-quantized priors:

\[
x_A=\text{VQAE proposal},\qquad
x_G^{\rm prior}=\text{VQGAN proposal}.
\]

Both proposals are converted to null-space residuals relative to the same anchor and projected back to the same measurement fiber.  The residual adapters then operate on the same base, anchor, split, architecture class, and deterministic geometry.

The matched prior training is important.  VQAE and VQGAN begin from the same autoencoder architecture and matched data/update schedule.  The VQGAN branch alone receives adversarial and discriminator-feature-matching terms during prior training.  Thus the VQGAN codebook and decoder are encouraged to represent locally realistic image modes rather than only a reconstruction average.  During the later GI pipeline the prior is frozen.  The measurement-conditioned refiner maps the same anchor and uncertainty estimate into that frozen latent space and decodes a proposal.

This gives the correct physical interpretation:

- the VQAE path tends to select a conservative, distortion-favorable coordinate on \(\mathcal F_y\);
- the VQGAN path supplies a different natural-image coordinate on \(\mathcal F_y\), with stronger edge/texture statistics but greater modal risk;
- neither path changes the measured component;
- their difference is a candidate direction inside the unmeasured ambiguity.

It is useful to speak informally of conditional-barycenter versus conditional-mode behavior, but no theorem here requires VQAE to be the exact conditional mean or VQGAN to be the exact MAP estimator.

### 2.3 The actual GAN mechanism

The selected B adapter has no discriminator.  Its generator objective contains pixel, structural, perceptual, and box terms, but its adversarial term is identically zero.  Nevertheless, the adapter is fed the direction induced by the pretrained VQGAN reconstruction.  The GAN is therefore substantive at the **proposal-prior level**, not at the **fusion-loss level**.

The causal statement supported by the four-cell matrix is:

> Adversarial pretraining changes the distribution of measurement-null proposal directions.  After the same non-adversarial adapter, fixed spectral operator, rank-one orthogonalization, and exact box-fiber projection, the VQGAN-derived directions align with the missing pixel and perceptual residuals more reliably than a matched independent VQAE-derived direction.

This is different from saying that an adversarial loss in the small adapter is necessary.  It is not.  Cell A and cell B are statistically indistinguishable at the level needed to justify the extra discriminator, so parsimony selects B.  Cell C shows why “two networks are diverse” is not an explanation: the second VQAE direction improves distortion but moves LPIPS in the wrong direction in every seed.  Cell D shows that the VQGAN displacement is not spectrally pure, while the direct high-pass advantage identifies where its comparative benefit is concentrated.

## 3. Final method as a physical coordinate decomposition

Let \(x_B\in\mathcal F_y\) be the stable measurement-consistent base.  Let \(c_S,c_G\in\ker A\) be the raw null corrections returned by the matched structural and VQGAN-source adapters.  Their difference

\[
d=c_G-c_S
\]

contains two conceptually distinct effects after filtering: a component that merely changes the amplitude of the structural correction, and a genuinely new coordinate.

Let \(H=H_{0.12,0.03}\) be the frozen smooth radial high-pass Fourier multiplier, and define the compressed physical operator

\[
K=P_NHP_N.
\]

Because the Fourier multiplier lies in \([0,1]\), \(H\) is self-adjoint, positive semidefinite, and nonexpansive.  Hence \(K\) is also self-adjoint, positive semidefinite, nonexpansive, and maps every input into \(\ker A\).

Set

\[
u=Kd.
\]

For \(c_S\neq 0\), decompose

\[
u=\beta c_S+v,\qquad
\beta=\frac{\langle u,c_S\rangle}{\|c_S\|_2^2},
\qquad
\langle v,c_S\rangle=0.
\]

Then the unorthogonalized correction would be

\[
c_S+\alpha u
=
(1+\alpha\beta)c_S+\alpha v.
\]

The first term silently retunes the already selected structural solution.  FOHI fixes the structural coefficient at one and retains only the second term.  No energy is restored after subtraction, because restoration would be a new amplitude rule and would destroy the nearest-point characterization below.

The raw-correction representative is retained deliberately.  EQ-FOHI tested the strongest endpoint-invariance objection and failed the predeclared direct LPIPS gate; the alpha-zero mismatch was negligible.  The raw formulation is therefore both simpler and empirically adequate.  This is not a claim that raw representatives are universally preferable; it is the frozen result of the one allowed falsification.

## 4. Minimal theorem package

Only three propositions are needed.

### Proposition 1 — exact physical consistency and box feasibility

Assume \(x_B\in\mathcal F_y\) and \(c_S,c_G\in\ker A\).  Let \(u=P_NHP_N(c_G-c_S)\) and let \(v\) be the FOHI direction.  Then, for every scalar \(\alpha\),

\[
A(x_B+c_S+\alpha v)=y.
\]

Moreover,

\[
\widehat x_\alpha
=
\Pi_{\mathcal F_y}(x_B+c_S+\alpha v)
\in\mathcal F_y.
\]

For every \(x^\star\in\mathcal F_y\),

\[
\|\widehat x_\alpha-x^\star\|_2^2
+
\|x_B+c_S+\alpha v-\widehat x_\alpha\|_2^2
\le
\|x_B+c_S+\alpha v-x^\star\|_2^2.
\]

**Proof.**  The two occurrences of \(P_N\) imply \(u\in\ker A\).  Since \(c_S\in\ker A\), subtracting a scalar multiple of \(c_S\) leaves \(v\in\ker A\).  The affine equality follows immediately.  The set \(\mathcal F_y\) is nonempty, closed, and convex, so its Euclidean projection exists uniquely and lies in the set.  The last inequality is the standard variational inequality for projection onto a closed convex set. \(\square\)

**Status.**  This is standard linear inverse-problem and convex-projection theory.  Its role is physical: the GAN contribution cannot alter a single ideal bucket measurement, and the final image obeys the intensity box.

The inequality guarantees contraction toward a clean truth in the same fiber relative to the particular preprojection proposal.  It does not, by itself, prove improvement over a separately projected structural arm in SSIM or LPIPS; those final-output comparisons remain empirical.

### Proposition 2 — unique minimum-change innovation

For \(c_S\neq0\), define

\[
\mathcal U_S=\ker A\cap c_S^\perp.
\]

Then

\[
\boxed{
v
=
\arg\min_{w\in\mathcal U_S}
\|w-u\|_2^2.
}
\]

For \(c_S=0\), \(\mathcal U_S=\ker A\) and \(v=u\).

**Proof.**  Since \(u,c_S\in\ker A\), the orthogonal projector from \(\ker A\) onto \(\ker A\cap c_S^\perp\) is the rank-one subtraction

\[
P_{\mathcal U_S}u
=
u-\frac{\langle u,c_S\rangle}{\|c_S\|_2^2}c_S.
\]

Orthogonal projection onto a closed subspace is the unique Euclidean nearest point. \(\square\)

An equivalent axiomatic statement is useful.  Once the frozen filtered direction \(u\) and structural coordinate \(c_S\) are given, FOHI is the unique rule satisfying all three conditions:

1. the admitted direction remains measurement-null;
2. it does not change the coefficient of \(c_S\);
3. it makes the smallest Euclidean change to \(u\).

Any nonidentity transformation after FOHI must abandon one of these conditions or introduce an additional metric or constraint.  The GI measurement cannot supply such a metric on \(\ker A\), because \(A^\top A\) vanishes there.  The Euclidean choice is also the one used by the final exact projection.

**Status.**  The rank-one projection theorem is standard.  The method-defining contribution is to impose structural-coordinate invariance on a filtered difference between two prior-selected points of the same GI fiber, and to test that isolation with matched causal source controls.

### Proposition 3 — proposal alignment is the risk mechanism

Let

\[
p_S=x_B+c_S,\qquad r^\star=x^\star-p_S,
\]

and let \(v\neq0\) be a fixed FOHI direction.  Before the final box projection, the per-pixel squared error along the FOHI line is

\[
D(\alpha)=\frac1n\|p_S+\alpha v-x^\star\|_2^2.
\]

Define

\[
a=\langle r^\star,v\rangle,\qquad b=\|v\|_2^2.
\]

Then

\[
D(\alpha)-D(0)
=
\frac{\alpha^2b-2\alpha a}{n}.
\]

For \(\alpha>0\), strict distortion improvement occurs exactly when

\[
a>0,\qquad
0<\alpha<\frac{2a}{b}.
\]

The line-optimal coefficient is \(a/b\).

For a proposal source \(q\), define the population alignment and energy

\[
A_q=\mathbb E\langle r^\star,v_q\rangle,
\qquad
B_q=\mathbb E\|v_q\|_2^2.
\]

Then

\[
\mathbb E[D_q(\alpha)-D(0)]
=
\frac{\alpha^2B_q-2\alpha A_q}{n}.
\]

A merely diverse second-network direction with \(A_q=0\) pays a strictly positive energy penalty.  Positive alignment, not diversity, is the causal quantity.

For simultaneous quality improvement, let

\[
\ell_{\rm pix}=\mathrm{MSE},\qquad
\ell_{\rm str}=1-\mathrm{SSIM},\qquad
\ell_{\rm perc}=\mathrm{LPIPS}.
\]

Suppose each loss is directionally smooth along \(v\):

\[
\ell_j(p_S+t v)
\le
\ell_j(p_S)
+t\,g_j
+\frac{L_j}{2}t^2\|v\|_2^2,
\qquad
g_j=\langle\nabla\ell_j(p_S),v\rangle.
\]

If

\[
g_{\rm pix}<0,\qquad
g_{\rm str}<0,\qquad
g_{\rm perc}<0,
\]

then all three losses improve for every

\[
0<\alpha<
\min_j
\frac{-2g_j}{L_j\|v\|_2^2}.
\]

For a local weighted feature distance
\(\|\phi(x)-\phi(x^\star)\|_W^2\), the first-order perceptual condition is

\[
\left\langle
J_\phi(p_S)v,\,
\phi(x^\star)-\phi(p_S)
\right\rangle_W>0.
\]

Thus a direction can improve PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS together when it lies in the intersection of their descent cones.  The VQGAN prior is useful only insofar as it changes the proposal distribution so that this intersection is entered more often.

**Proof.**  The MSE identity follows by expanding the square.  The joint result follows by applying the directional quadratic upper bound to each loss and intersecting the resulting positive step intervals. \(\square\)

**Status.**  The expansion and descent lemma are standard.  The genuinely useful combined principle is the causal identification of a pretrained adversarial prior with a favorable distribution of **fiber-orthogonal innovation directions**, while the fusion adapter itself remains non-adversarial.

## 5. What is genuinely new enough for the paper

The paper should not sell a new theorem about orthogonal projection.  Its strongest defensible contribution is the following combined physical principle:

> In an underdetermined ghost-imaging inverse problem, a distortion-oriented reconstruction and an adversarially pretrained reconstruction are alternative prior-selected coordinates on the same measurement fiber.  The incremental value of the adversarial prior can be isolated by retaining the high-frequency-dominant null displacement, quotienting out the structural coordinate by the unique minimum-change rank-one projection, and enforcing the exact box-fiber constraint.  Matched source and loss controls then distinguish adversarial-prior alignment from generic ensembling or an adversarial fusion loss.

The ingredients that are standard and should be labeled as such are:

- row-space/null-space decomposition;
- Euclidean projection onto a closed convex set;
- rank-one Hilbert-space projection;
- quadratic risk expansion and local descent bounds.

The journal-level novelty is their sparse combination into a physically interpretable GAN+GI method, together with the four-cell causal design, rate boundary, and independent-operator evidence.

Claims that must not be made:

- the VQGAN recovers additional measured photons;
- null-space membership proves semantic correctness;
- the small residual adapter needs adversarial training;
- high-pass is the only beneficial band;
- the method is robust at 2% sampling;
- three random seeds establish universality across operator families;
- the simulation establishes hardware, photon, background, or calibration robustness;
- exact equality to a noisy measurement is statistically correct;
- the held-out test supports any claim before its one authorized opening.

## 6. Interpretation of the rate dependence

The rate result is consistent with, but does not prove, a posterior-concentration interpretation.

At 10% sampling the measured row space is larger and the admissible fiber is narrower.  The stable base and structural coordinate are better constrained, so the remaining VQGAN displacement is less likely to select a wrong texture mode.  The selected B arm is consequently 3/3 positive with substantially larger gains.

At 5%, the method is also stable and has the strongest causal and operator-seed evidence.

At 2%, one seed still improves PSNR and SSIM strongly but worsens LPIPS with an entirely unfavorable interval.  The correct reading is not “the average still works.”  It is that extreme undersampling leaves enough unmeasured freedom for the VQGAN proposal to become perceptually misaligned in a seed-dependent way.  The robust operating envelope therefore begins at 5% for the present method and data.

## 7. Final validation and claim boundary

### 7.1 Claims supported before the held-out opening

At 5% sampling, the current validation evidence supports:

- simultaneous PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS improvement over the matched structural arm;
- robustness across three independent training/proposal pairings;
- robustness across three predeclared, independently seeded, distinct matrices from the same structured GI operator family;
- exact box and measurement consistency for every evaluated reconstruction.

At 10% sampling, it supports:

- simultaneous improvement in all three metrics across three training lanes for the tested 10% operator configuration;
- a moderate-rate simulation claim;
- no operator-seed-generalization claim yet at 10%.

At 2% sampling, it supports only a boundary statement:

- distortion improvements can persist;
- perceptual alignment is not seed-stable;
- 2% is not part of the confirmatory operating envelope.

The three independent 5% operators are sufficient for the narrow statement “robust across independent seeds of the tested structured operator family in simulation.”  They are not sufficient for arbitrary masks, a different pattern family, a physical single-pixel camera, or unknown calibration.

### 7.2 Noise is not a prerequisite for the held-out test

The primary claim is explicitly noiseless simulation.  A statistically correct noisy extension would replace exact equality by a confidence or likelihood fiber, for example

\[
\mathcal F_{y,\delta}
=
\left\{
x\in[0,1]^n:
(Ax-y_{\rm obs})^\top\Sigma^{-1}(Ax-y_{\rm obs})
\le \chi^2_{r,1-\delta}
\right\}
\]

for a Gaussian model, or by a fixed Poisson-deviance set.

That extension is not required before the held-out test and must not be allowed to delay or modify the frozen main method.  It is a separate preregistered secondary simulation study.  Any result obtained by enforcing \(Ax=y_{\rm obs}\) under noisy observations should be called an algorithmic stress test, not statistically correct noisy consistency.

### 8.6 Familywise decision rule

The headline confirmatory claim is a single six-component intersection-union claim:

\[
\begin{aligned}
&\Delta{\rm PSNR}_{5}>0,\quad
\Delta{\rm SSIM}_{5}>0,\quad
\Delta{\rm LPIPS}_{5}<0,\\
&\Delta{\rm PSNR}_{10}>0,\quad
\Delta{\rm SSIM}_{10}>0,\quad
\Delta{\rm LPIPS}_{10}<0.
\end{aligned}
\]

Use the predeclared two-sided 95% hierarchical bootstrap intervals.  The headline succeeds only if all six intervals exclude zero in the favorable direction, every 5% operator lane has favorable mean signs, every 10% training lane has favorable mean signs, and all feasibility gates pass.  Requiring every component to pass is an intersection-union test and controls the false declaration probability for the conjunction at no more than 0.05.

For any separate metric-wise or rate-wise significance language, also compute one-sided paired-bootstrap \(p\)-values and apply Holm correction across the six rate-by-metric components.  Descriptive means and unadjusted intervals may still be reported, but they cannot support an individual confirmatory claim if the Holm-adjusted test fails.
