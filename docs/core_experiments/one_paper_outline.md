# One-Paper Outline

Working title: Auditing Learned Ghost Imaging: Measurement Certificates, Their Boundaries, and Prior-Supplied Content

1. Introduction: low-sampling GI needs both visual quality and bucket-signal accountability.
2. Measurement audit: define Pi_y^lambda, x_data=D(y), RelMeasErr convention, and exact-A/split provenance.
3. Certificate behavior: post-hoc audit, range/share law, lambda frontier, modal contraction.
4. Boundary: wrong-y/shuffle/A-drift and feasible cross-class images show measurement consistency is not semantic correctness.
5. Prior-supplied content: GAN as a high-capacity prior case study, P0 xhat maps, alpha trust knob.
6. Safety and regimes: gauge shortcut stress, standard-cGAN comparability, 5% positive vs 10% weak gate.
7. Limitations: no semantic certificate, no SOTA, no diffusion/hardware, human preference not collected.
8. Conclusion: auditable learned reconstruction is a certificate plus a declared boundary, not a guarantee of truth.
