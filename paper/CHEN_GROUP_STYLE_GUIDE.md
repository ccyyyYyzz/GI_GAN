# Chen Wen (PolyU) group — paper-writing style guide

_Derived 2026-07-05 from 19 group PDFs (Chen/Zhou/Hao/Xiao/Song/Peng/Jiao/Wang; Optics Express / Optics Letters / APL / APL Photonics / Adv. Imaging). For the OPTICS-FIRST version of our paper (Optics Express / Optics & Lasers in Eng., where Wen Chen is an editor). Our cross-modality TMLR/TCI version keeps its terse voice._

## Style in one paragraph

The Chen-group (HK PolyU Photonics) house style is expository, formulaic, and reader-flattering rather than terse or measurement-theoretic. Across all five profiles the register is remarkably consistent. (1) FIELD-FIRST FRAMING: abstracts and intros open by elevating the whole subfield with a broad, mildly evaluative sentence ("Optical encryption has provided a new insight for securing information"; "Single-pixel imaging has become one of the most interesting and promising imaging technologies"), then pivot on a hard "However," / "Despite ... remains challenging" that names one concrete unsolved obstacle, then land the contribution with a fixed launch phrase. (2) FIXED CONTRIBUTION FRAME: "In this paper/Letter, we report/propose [named method + acronym] to [correct/estimate/eliminate/realize] ... through [hard media]," with novelty stamped by "for the first time to our knowledge" / "to the best of our knowledge" (a hedge in form used to make a strong priority claim). (3) SELF-REFERENCE AS "THE PROPOSED METHOD": the group's own contribution is almost never "our method"; it is "the proposed method / the developed system / the designed scheme / the proposed algorithm," repeated many times per paper, and each proposed method is given a coined acronym (TCGI, SCGI, DP3, GCA). (4) HEAVY PASSIVE, IMPERSONAL VOICE: methods and results are agentless ("an iterative algorithm is developed," "experiments are conducted," "it is demonstrated/verified/illustrated that"), with "we" reserved for contribution and conclusion sentences only. (5) STRONG CLAIM + RITUAL HEDGE: own results are asserted flatly and confidently, often with universal markers ("always," "totally cannot work," "high robustness is validated"), while prior work and outlook are softened ("may be limited," "could," "it can be believed that"). (6) EQUATION-AND-GLOSS METHODS: every display equation is introduced by "... can be described by / can be obtained by" and immediately unpacked by a "where X denotes ..., and Y denotes ..." glossary sentence; procedures run as explicit numbered/sequential steps. (7) FIGURE-BY-FIGURE, RESPECTIVELY-BOUND RESULTS: results are narrated panel-by-panel as a fixed baseline -> partial -> proposed comparison, with per-panel metrics (PSNR/CNR/SSIM/CC) quoted inline to one or two decimals and bound to panel order by the signature connective "respectively." (8) OPTIMISTIC, GENERALIZING CLOSE: conclusions restate the abstract in present-perfect ("We have reported ..."), reaffirm feasibility/effectiveness/robustness, and end on a forward-looking outlook built from stock phrases ("opens up an avenue for ...," "could open up a new/different research perspective for ...," "paves the way for ..."); there is usually no separate limitations paragraph. (9) SIGNATURE PROBLEM FRAMING: "dynamic and complex/turbid scattering media" + "harsh/complex environments" recurs verbatim in title, abstract, intro, and conclusion, and dense self-citation situates each paper in an ongoing program. Recurring discourse markers: "It is worth noting that," "It should be emphasized that," "For the sake of brevity," "For simplicity," "Therefore," "Hence," "Subsequently," "Similarly," "It could be believed that." Concrete hardware is dumped with brand+model in parentheses (Holoeye LC-R720; Thorlabs PDA100A2) and physical quantities carry one decimal and explicit units (532.0 nm, 800.0 rpm).

## Abstract template (their register)

[Field/technique + setting] has become an attractive/promising approach for [broad application area]. [One-sentence virtue or capability statement.] However, [named concrete obstacle in the hard regime, e.g. through dynamic and complex scattering media / at low sampling rates] remains challenging, and [specific failure of prior work: distorted measurements / needs prior knowledge / vulnerability]. Here, we report [named method (ACRONYM)] that [core mechanism: eliminates/corrects/estimates X] to achieve [headline benefit: high-quality/high-fidelity/high-robustness reconstruction]. In the proposed method, [one-sentence mechanism: an audit/algorithm is developed to <verb> the <quantity>], for the first time to our knowledge. It is experimentally/numerically demonstrated that [headline quantitative result with one number + unit] is achieved, and high feasibility, effectiveness, and robustness are validated. The proposed method opens up an avenue for [forward-looking application in complex/harsh environments].

## Introduction arc

Beat 1 (field elevation + lineage): open with a broad textbook sentence on why the technique matters ("Optical imaging has become an important tool in many fields, such as ...") and trace lineage to a seminal/pioneering work with a dense bracketed citation range; list downstream variants and application areas as comma-separated "e.g., ..., and ..." catalogs, each item carrying its own citation. Beat 2 (name the physical obstacle in plain mechanistic terms): state the mechanism of failure concretely ("the light is scattered into unpredictable paths, breaking the point-to-point mapping"; "at low sampling rates the inverse problem is deeply underdetermined"). Beat 3 (mini-catalogue of prior fixes, each undercut): introduce each existing method family (transmission matrix, wavefront shaping, memory effect, adaptive optics, compressed sensing) then immediately undercut it with a one-clause limitation ("However, it requires prior knowledge / works only in static media / needs a guide star"). Beat 4 (state the residual gap as a demand): "Therefore, it is desirable/crucial to develop [the capability] in [dynamic and complex scattering environments]." Beat 5 (contribution paragraph reusing the abstract wording): "In this paper, we report [named method + acronym] to [verb] ... through [hard media]." Itemize the pieces with "A ... algorithm is developed," "A ... method is designed," and, if enumerating advantages, "Firstly, ... Secondly, ... Thirdly, ...". Close the intro by previewing the experimental validation and the paper's organization.

## Figure-caption rules

Lead with a short noun-phrase label, NOT a full sentence and NOT a bold lead-in: "Fig. N. Schematic of an optical setup for the proposed ..." / "A flow chart of the proposed ..." / "Experimental results:". Follow the label with a colon or period, then an inline glossary spelling out EVERY symbol and component abbreviation, separated by semicolons or em-dashes: "SLM: spatial light modulator; BD: bucket detector; BS: beam splitter; M: random mask." For multi-panel figures, exhaustively enumerate each sub-label and map it to its content/condition/method "(a)-(c) reconstruction by conventional GI; (d)-(f) by the proposed method." Embed the actual quantitative results inside the caption when they anchor the panel, bound by "respectively": "PSNR values are 37.60 dB, 35.32 dB, and 34.10 dB, respectively." Define any in-panel math operator at the end of the caption ("The symbol delta denotes subtracting an average value from the intensities"). Add housekeeping lines where relevant ("The color is used only for display purposes"; "a neutral density filter is omitted for brevity"). Name equipment with brand and model in parentheses and one-decimal physical quantities.

## Terminology map (ours → theirs)

- our-term 'measurement operator A' -> their-term 'the sensing/measurement operator; the optical channel' (frame A physically, not just algebraically)
- 'range/row space' and 'null space' -> keep the math terms but gloss them as 'the measured component' and 'the unmeasured (prior-supplied) component' on first use
- 'measurement audit Pi(v)' -> 'the proposed measurement-consistency correction/audit (name it, e.g. a range-null audit, and give it an acronym)'
- 'our method / the audit' -> 'the proposed method / the proposed audit / the developed scheme' (their standardized self-reference)
- 'RelMeasErr / measurement residual' -> keep, but pair with a named metric and report 'respectively'-bound per-row values; add CNR/SSIM alongside PSNR where an optics reader expects them
- 'ghost imaging / single-pixel imaging' -> spell out acronym on first use: 'ghost imaging (GI)', 'single-pixel imaging (SPI)', 'single-pixel/bucket detector'
- 'sensing patterns / rows of A' -> 'illumination patterns'; measurements -> 'single-pixel light intensities / realizations'
- 'low sampling rate 5-10%' -> 'low sampling ratio'; 'undersampled' -> 'compressive'
- 'hallucination / feasible wrong image' -> keep 'measurement-consistent but semantically wrong', but frame as the 'verifiability barrier' and, for their audience, tie to 'reconstruction fidelity in complex/harsh imaging conditions'
- 'certificate / accountability' -> gloss as 'a ground-truth-free reliability/consistency indicator'; avoid pure crypto-flavored 'certificate' without an optics gloss
- 'GAN branch' -> 'a representative generative prior'; keep the caveat but phrase advantages with 'Firstly/Secondly'
- signature framing to adopt: 'high-fidelity/high-robustness', 'the proposed method opens up an avenue for ...', 'for the first time to our knowledge', 'it is demonstrated that'

## DO

- Open the abstract with a one-line field-level premise about GI/SPI imaging before the technical claim, then pivot on 'However,' to the concrete gap (which content is measured vs prior-supplied at low sampling ratios).
- Give the audit a name and acronym and refer to it consistently as 'the proposed audit / the proposed method' throughout.
- Spell out every acronym on first use with the expansion followed by the acronym in parentheses; keep GI, SPI, PSNR, CNR, SSIM explicit.
- Introduce each display equation with 'can be described by / can be obtained by' and follow it with a 'where X denotes ..., Y denotes ...' glossary sentence (the manuscript already glosses symbols; make the trailing where-clause explicit).
- Report per-row/per-panel metrics bound with 'respectively', and add CNR or SSIM alongside PSNR and RelMeasErr so an optics reader sees familiar image-quality metrics.
- Rewrite figure captions as noun-phrase labels + semicolon symbol glossaries + embedded numbers, replacing bold lead-ins like '**The synthesis.**'.
- Frame results as an explicit baseline -> partial-correction -> proposed comparison (BP/Tikhonov/CS-TV -> ablation -> full audited output), which the tables already support.
- Close with a present-perfect recap ('We have reported ...') plus a forward-looking outlook sentence ('opens up an avenue for reliable GI/SPI in complex, low-sampling-ratio conditions').
- Name hardware/simulation parameters with vendor/model and one-decimal units where any real or emulated optical setup is described.
- Add a short field-elevation + prior-fix-catalogue funnel to the introduction before the geometric route, situating the range-null view against transmission-matrix / compressed-sensing / learned-prior GI-SPI literature with dense citations.

## DON'T (keep our rigor)

- Do not gut the rigor: keep the exact projector algebra, the contraction proof, the PSNR-ceiling theorem, and the honest calibration/negative results (their omission of limitations is a habit to resist, not adopt).
- Do not open with the bare aphorism 'measurement consistency is not evidence of image correctness' as the first sentence for an optics venue; lead with the field premise, then deliver the aphorism as the 'However' pivot.
- Do not overclaim with their universal markers ('always', 'totally cannot work', 'high security is achieved') for our results — our contribution is precisely a limits/barrier paper, so keep claims bounded to the reported rows.
- Do not drop the explicit caveats about the GAN branch, CS-TV small-subset evidence, simulation-scoped operator drift, or unresolved calibration — rephrase them softly rather than deleting them.
- Do not adopt the crypto spellings/idioms ('cryptoanalysis', 'plaintext/ciphertext') — they belong to the encryption sub-line, not our imaging paper.
- Do not let 'respectively'/'the proposed method' repetition become verbatim copy-paste across abstract/intro/conclusion to the point of dulling the measurement-theoretic core; use the templates for framing, keep the math prose sharp.
- Do not convert honest en-dashed caveats into unconditional optimism; keep the verifiability barrier stated plainly even inside an optics-first frame.
- Do not remove the ground-truth-free framing (their metrics assume GT); our GT-free certificate is a genuine differentiator to foreground, not hide.

## Validation against the full 19-paper corpus (close read, 2026-07-05)

A close read of all 19 group PDFs (Chen/Hao/Peng/Song/Wang/Xiao/Zhou/Jiao; IEEE Photonics J., Optics Express/Letters, APL/APL Photonics, ACS Photonics, Optics & Lasers in Eng., Advanced Imaging) confirms this guide is ~90% accurate. **Confirmed at 100% across the corpus:** field-first opener + "However"/"Despite … remains challenging" pivot; the "In this paper/Letter, we report/propose [method + ACRONYM]" contribution frame (18/19); "the proposed method / developed scheme / designed algorithm" self-reference, each with a coined acronym (TCGI, SCGI, DP3, GCA, URED); heavy agentless passive ("an algorithm is developed", "it is demonstrated that"); equation-and-gloss ("can be described by" + trailing where-clause, 100%); figure-by-figure "respectively"-bound per-panel metrics (PSNR/CNR/SSIM/CC); present-perfect recap + "opens up an avenue for …" close; "dynamic and complex scattering media" / "harsh/complex environments" signature framing; one-decimal hardware (532.0 nm, brand+model). **Two corrections to this guide from the evidence:** (1) A THIRD register exists — the optical-encryption line (Zhou 2019–2020, three papers) uses the *identical* structure but swaps entirely to DRPE / plaintext / ciphertext / ML-attack / cryptanalysis vocabulary. It is a sibling register, not our imaging register; do NOT import its terms. (2) The "usually no separate limitations paragraph" habit (item 8) holds for the empirical Letters but NOT for the group's reviews (Jiao 2026, Xiao 2025), which carry explicit future-perspectives sections — so our barrier paper's dedicated Scope/limitations section is genre-consistent for a limits/review-flavored paper, not a deviation to hide. **Terminology conventions to match (imaging register):** "single-pixel light intensities / realizations", "illumination patterns", "bucket / single-pixel detector", "sampling ratio" (never "rate"), "the proposed method (ACRONYM)", "measurement-consistent", "high-fidelity/high-quality reconstruction", "compressive imaging / compressed sensing". Our OPTICS_DRAFT already adopts every confirmed trait; the close read drove only small terminology fixes (CASSI expansion unified to "coded-aperture snapshot spectral imaging"; "sensing operator" standardized; the MCA acronym now coined-and-reused per house habit; the Tikhonov filter-factor complement glossed for the optics audience). The paper's measurement-theoretic voice (explicit range/null-space characterization) is a genuine differentiator — only 2–3 corpus papers touch data-consistency language — and should stay foregrounded, not diluted toward their qualitative-recovery register.
