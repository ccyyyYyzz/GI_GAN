## 1. Motivation and the gap this fills

Our impossibility/certificate/dial results are currently demonstrated on MRI-style operators. The ghost-imaging / single-pixel community, however, frames its own open problem as *quality and speed and generalization* — never as *which recovered content the measurement can certify*. This is stated by the field's own reviews: Gibson, Johnson & Padgett (2020) cast reconstruction as tuning `χ²/M + λR(O)` until `χ²/M ≈ 1`, and Song et al. (2025) list interpretability and generalization as unresolved liabilities while cataloguing ever-larger DL reconstructors — but neither asks whether invented content is verifiable from the bucket signal.

So a reviewer from that community can grant our MRI results yet ask: *does this say anything in my modality?* IP-11 answers by running the **identical** ground-truth-free audit across the canonical GI reconstruction families — correlation-GI, CS-GI, and learned GI (GIDL) — on **one shared set of bucket measurements**, and showing:

1. the **measured (range) modes contract identically** across all three reconstructors (the certificate is a property of the operator, not the reconstructor); and
2. the **null-space content — and the true error — differ wildly** across them (each reconstructor's "detail" at low sampling is an invented, uncertified choice).

That is "certify what you measure" made **reconstructor-agnostic in the reader's own modality**, and "govern what you cannot" made vivid by the documented GI failure mode where a digit *2* is reconstructed as a *3* and fabricated stripes appear.

**Why this is credible, not just a re-skin of the MRI experiment:** the GI/single-pixel forward model *is literally* our operator. Shapiro's computational GI (2008) and Bromberg–Katz–Silberberg's single-detector GI (2009) reduce to `y = A x` with **known, computed** modulation rows `φ_m` (SLM/DMD patterns), a single bucket integrating `y[m] = ⟨x, φ_m⟩`, and `m ≪ n` (undersampled). Because the reference arm is computed rather than physically measured, the operator `A` is fully known at test time, so `P_R = A†A` and `P_0 = I − A†A` are exactly computable — the precise regime our certificate needs. Landau's (1967) density theorem guarantees the sub-Nyquist bucket count leaves a provably nontrivial null space, so the barrier is not an artifact of any one reconstructor.

## 2. Minimal experiment design (buildable from cheap GI baselines)

### 2.1 Shared measurement (built once, reused by every reconstructor)

- **Signal set.** MNIST digits at `img_size = 32` (`n = 1024`), matching the exact setting of GIDL (Lyu et al. 2017) and GICNN (He et al. 2018) so our baselines reproduce their published regime; optionally a second binary object (double-slit / Hebrew-letter aleph) to match Katz et al. (2009).
- **Operator `A ∈ ℝ^{m×n}`.** Speckle/pattern rows `φ_m` from three standard GI mask families to show operator-generality: (i) random binary/Bernoulli speckle (pseudothermal GI), (ii) Hadamard, (iii) DCT-low-frequency. In this repo these are already available via `build_dc_balanced_rows(family, m-1, dim=n, img_size, seed)` and `dct_lowfreq_non_dc_rows`, with DC handled by the existing DC-balanced construction. Normalize to orthonormal rows (repo's `matrix_normalization="orthonormal_rows"`) so the certificate's per-mode form is clean.
- **Sampling ratios `β = m/n`.** `β ∈ {0.05, 0.10, 0.20}` — the exact low-sampling band where GIDL claims recognizable digits and CS-GI is documented to collapse (`β = 0.05`). This is where the null space is largest and the invented-content story is sharpest.
- **Bucket record.** `y = A x_gt` (add optional Gaussian detector noise to reproduce the GIDL/CS-GI robustness comparison). One `y` per image is the **single shared record** all reconstructors must explain.

### 2.2 The three GI reconstructors (the only new code needed)

All three consume the *same* `y` and the *same* `A`; they differ only in how they fill the null space.

| Reconstructor | Rule | Null-space fill | Repo status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Correlation-GI** | `x̂_corr = A^T (y − ⟨y⟩)` (second-order correlation / adjoint), per Shapiro 2008, Bromberg 2009 | implicit, spectrally flat / noisy | new (≈10 lines: adjoint + DC subtract) |
| **CS-GI** | `min ‖Ψ x‖₁ s.t. A x = y` (2D-DCT sparsity, TVAL3/FISTA), per Katz et al. 2009 | sparse-prior guess | new (wrap an existing L1/TV solver) |
| **GIDL** | plain DNN mapping correlation image → clean digit, per Lyu et al. 2017 | trained MNIST prior | new (small FC net, 2000-pair MNIST train) — or substitute the repo's learned reconstructor as a stand-in |

*Implementation note.* GIDL is literally *reconstruct-then-refine* (`x̂_corr → DNN`), so our audit drops in **between the two steps** and again on the final output, exactly as flagged in card `Lyu_2017` (`innovation_spark`). No baseline needs to beat any other; the point is the audit, not a leaderboard.

### 2.3 The audit (already implemented in this repo — reuse verbatim)

For each reconstructor output `x̂` and the shared `y`, using `src/projections.py`:

- **Range/null split.** `x̂_R = exact_row_project(x̂, op)`, `x̂_0 = exact_null_project(x̂, op)` — the exact matrix-free `P_R = A^T(AA^T)^† A`, `P_0 = I − P_R` (float64, Cholesky/pinv fallback with logged conditioning).
- **Measured-mode certificate.** The exact per-mode contraction `λ/(λ+σ_i²)` from the SVD of `A` (the same operator for all three reconstructors ⇒ **identical certificate**). Report the modal spectrum, not a scalar.
- **Data-consistency / feasible-wrong number.** `RelMeasErr(x̂) = ‖A x̂ − y‖ / ‖y‖` via `relative_measurement_error`. For any reconstructor that fills only the null space (or after projecting each output through `exact_audit(x̂, y, op)`), this is `~2e-15` — machine precision — while the null content, and therefore the true error `‖x̂ − x_gt‖`, diverges across reconstructors.
- **Null energy.** `‖x̂_0‖² / ‖x̂‖²` — the fraction of each reconstruction that the measurement provably cannot certify.

### 2.4 The single headline figure / table

**Per `β`, one row per reconstructor:**

| Reconstructor | Certificate spectrum `λ/(λ+σ_i²)` | RelMeasErr (measured ledger) | Null energy `‖x̂_0‖²/‖x̂‖²` (uncertified ledger) | PSNR/SSIM vs GT (quality) | True error `‖x̂−x_gt‖` |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Correlation-GI | **identical** across the column | ~2e-15 after audit* | large, distinct | low | large |
| CS-GI | **identical** | ~2e-15 after audit* | different | medium | medium |
| GIDL | **identical** | ~2e-15 after audit* | different (trained prior) | high-looking | can be wrong (2→3) |

\*The raw learned/CS outputs need not be data-consistent; the audited/projected variant is. The **contrast we sell is the invariant certificate column vs the divergent null-energy/true-error columns** — quality (PSNR/SSIM) and accountability (null energy) move independently.

**The money sentence for the caption:** *On one bucket record, three canonical GI reconstructors contract the measured modes by the exact same `λ/(λ+σ_i²)` spectrum, yet disagree in the null space by up to [X]% of their energy — so the digit each of them "sees" below the sampling limit is invented, not measured.*

## 3. Proposition (GI-native converse) + proof sketch

**Proposition (GI feasible-wrong twin).** *Let `A ∈ ℝ^{m×n}` be a computational-GI modulation operator with `m < n` (sub-Nyquist bucket count) and let `y = A x_gt` be a bucket record. Then for any `v` with `A v = 0` (i.e. `v ∈ Null(A) = range(P_0)`), the image `x' = x_gt + v` produces the identical bucket record `A x' = y` to machine precision, while `‖x' − x_gt‖ = ‖v‖` can be made arbitrarily large. Consequently no bucket record from a single fixed GI operator can distinguish `x_gt` from `x'`, and any reconstructor's choice among them is a null-space prior the measurement cannot certify.*

**Proof sketch.** `A x' = A x_gt + A v = y + 0 = y`; the residual `‖A x' − y‖ = ‖A v‖ = 0` up to floating-point (`~2e-15` in float64, matching our measured witness). By construction `x_gt` and `x'` are *observationally equivalent* for the operator `A` — the imaging instance of Rothenberg's (1971) singular-information direction and Manski's (2003) non-refutable region: the measurement can never refute the null component `v`. That `Null(A)` is nontrivial for `m < n` is exactly Landau's (1967) density floor and is stated verbatim in the GI/CS-GI literature ("since `M < N` … infinitely many `x'` satisfy `Φ x' = y`", Duarte et al. 2008; Katz et al. 2009). Choosing `v` to route through `P_0 = I − A†A` (repo `exact_null_project`) yields a *constructive* GI feasible-wrong twin, upgrading the existence statement to a reproducible per-instance object. ∎

**Honesty guard.** The proposition asserts only that the bucket cannot *distinguish* the twins; it makes **no** claim that any particular filled `x'` is correct. Every reconstructor in §2.2 picks one such `x'`; the certificate reports only the shared, measured range component as accountable and labels the rest invented.
