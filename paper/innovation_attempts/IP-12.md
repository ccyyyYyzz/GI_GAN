# IP-12 — Live "consistency ≠ correctness" audits on the strongest modern solvers

**Status: proposed** (needs external solver code — DDNM / DPS / DDRM / MCG released repos).
This file is a paper-ready subsection + a minimal PoC design + a proposition with proof-sketch,
all grounded in the reading cards. Nothing here claims the measurement certifies invented content.

Cards used (by repository file / R-number, the ID scheme in `GI99_reading_cards.json`):
- **R026 — DDNM** (Wang, Yu & Zhang, ICLR 2023): range fixed to `A†y`, diffusion fills **only** the null space, `A x̂ = y` **exactly** for any linear `A`.
- **R070 — DDRM** (Kawar, Elad, Ermon, Song, NeurIPS 2022): diffuses in the SVD spectral space of `H`; `s_i = 0` (null) modes synthesized from the prior, `s_i > 0` modes blended with `ȳ` by weight `η_b < 1`.
- **R071 — DPS** (Chung, Kim, McCann, Klasky, Ye, ICLR 2023): a **soft** likelihood-gradient step `−ζ_i ∇‖y − A(x̂₀)‖²`; no hard projection, so `A x̂ = y` does **not** hold exactly.
- **R074 — MCG** (Chung, Sim, Ryu, Ye, NeurIPS 2022): writes `A = I − PᵀP` (our `P_0`) so measured coords are pinned to `y` and **only** the orthogonal complement (null space) evolves from the prior.
- Supporting: **R075 — DiffPIR** (soft proximal data step, `ρ_t`), **R066 — PULSE** (feasible-but-wrong under a downsampling `A`), **R035 — DynamicDPS** (uses the same `I − A⁺A` range/null split, calls it intrinsic/extrinsic hallucination).
- Anchors already differentiated (see `NOVELTY_THREAT_DIFFERENTIATION.md`): **Gottschling 2025** (Troublesome Kernel) + **Iagaru et al. 2605.13146** (assessment/limits, no exact per-mode cert, no witness, no dial).

## 1. Paper subsection (drop-in for P1 + P4)

> ### Auditing the strongest solvers: exact data consistency is exactly what our certificate expects
>
> The modern generative inverse-problem solvers are often read as counter-evidence to an
> impossibility stance: DDNM, DDRM, and MCG restore images that satisfy the measurement to
> machine precision, and DPS and DiffPIR reach near-SOTA perceptual quality. We take the
> opposite reading. Precisely **because** these solvers are strong, running our test-time audit on
> their outputs turns each of them into a live exhibit of "consistency ≠ correctness" (P1) and of
> ungoverned null-space supply (P4) — *without contesting their reconstruction quality*.
>
> The families split cleanly by how they treat the range space, and the split is decided by their
> **own published construction**, not by us:
>
> - **Exact-range solvers (DDNM R026, MCG R074).** DDNM pins the range component to `A†y` and lets
>   a diffusion prior generate only the null component `(I − A†A) x̄`; MCG literally sets its update
>   operator to `A = I − PᵀP = P_0`, so measured coordinates are fixed by `y` while only the
>   orthogonal complement evolves. Their outputs satisfy `A x̂ = y` to numerical precision — the same
>   regime as our governed dial. Our certificate therefore reports, for every measured singular
>   mode, an exact contraction `λ/(λ+σ_i²) ≈ 1`: their measured ledger is fully accounted for. The
>   catch is that this is **all** the measurement can vouch for. The visually decisive content — the
>   texture the reader trusts — lives entirely in `(I − A†A) x̄`, which by `A P_0 = 0` leaves the
>   record unchanged and is therefore provably unverifiable from `y`.
> - **Spectral-blend solver (DDRM R070).** DDRM synthesizes `s_i = 0` modes from the prior and blends
>   measured modes with weight `η_b < 1`, so `H x̂ = y` holds only up to that blend. Our per-mode
>   audit exposes this directly: the measured-mode contraction departs from `1` by exactly the amount
>   `η_b` relaxes fidelity — a hidden accountability cost invisible to PSNR/FID.
> - **Soft-consistency solvers (DPS R071, DiffPIR R075).** A single likelihood gradient (DPS) or a
>   proximal balance `ρ_t` (DiffPIR) enforces consistency only softly, so their outputs do **not**
>   satisfy `A x̂ = y` exactly and carry unconstrained, unaudited null content. Here the certificate
>   quantifies two separate leaks at once: a nonzero **range** residual `RelMeasErr = ‖A x̂ − y‖/‖y‖`
>   (measured modes not fully honoured) *and* the uncertified null fill.
>
> For any of them we can further exhibit the converse **constructively**: given a solver output `x̂`
> with measurement `y = A x̂` (exact-range case) or the record's own `y`, we build a **feasible-wrong
> twin** `x̂' = x̂ + P_0 v` that reproduces the identical record to ~1e-15 — tighter than the noisy
> ground truth — yet is a different, visibly wrong scene. The measurement cannot distinguish `x̂` from
> `x̂'`; the certificate assigns them identical measured-mode contractions and localizes their entire
> disagreement to the provably-unverifiable null space. Finally, swapping the solver's own null fill
> for our governed VQAE+VQGAN B-fusion under the **same** `P_0` yields an exactly-consistent variant
> (`A x̂_B = y` for every `B`) — converting an implicit, unaudited fill into a metered, certifiable
> one. We do not claim to beat these solvers on quality; we certify what their measurement can and
> cannot vouch for, and govern the remainder.

## 2. Proposition (feasible-wrong twin transfers to any exact-range solver)

**Setup.** Let `A ∈ ℝ^{m×n}` be a linear forward operator with `m < n` (nontrivial kernel), and let
`P_0 = I − A†A` be the orthogonal projector onto `Null(A)` (so `A P_0 = 0`). Let `x̂` be the output of
an exact-range solver `S ∈ {DDNM (R026), MCG (R074)}` on record `y`, i.e. `A x̂ = y`.

**Proposition (transfer of the converse).** For **every** solver output `x̂` with `A x̂ = y` and every
`v ∈ ℝ^n` with `P_0 v ≠ 0`, the twin `x̂' := x̂ + P_0 v` satisfies

1. `A x̂' = y` (exact feasibility, identical record), and
2. `x̂' ≠ x̂` with `‖x̂' − x̂‖ = ‖P_0 v‖ > 0` (a genuinely different image),

so `x̂` is **not identifiable** from `y`: the solver certifies only the range component
`A†A x̂ = A†y`, shared by the entire feasible family `{x̂ + P_0 v}`. Consequently our per-mode
certificate assigns `x̂` and `x̂'` **identical** contractions `λ/(λ+σ_i²)` on all measured modes, and
their `RelMeasErr = ‖A x̂ − y‖/‖y‖` are equal (≈ machine precision).

**Proof-sketch.** `A x̂' = A x̂ + A P_0 v = y + 0 = y` gives (1). Orthogonality of `P_0` gives (2).
Both twins have the same range projection `A†A x̂`, so any operator that is a function of `(A, y)`
alone — including the certificate `Π_y^λ` and `RelMeasErr` — returns the same value on both. ∎

**Honesty red line.** The proposition is a statement **against** over-claiming: it shows the
measurement certifies `x̂` and `x̂'` equally, hence certifies *neither's* null content. To realize
a *named* wrong twin (not just "some" `v`), pick a different-class scene `x_j` and set
`x̂' = A†y + P_0 x_j` (equivalently `x̂' = audit_flat(flatten(x_j), y)` in repo terms, §3): the twin
carries `x_j`'s null-space semantics while matching `y` exactly. This is the exact construction
already realized on our own operator in `experiments_feasible_wrong.py` (median `RelMeasErr ≈` machine
precision); IP-12 proposes to run the **same** construction on released-solver outputs.

## 3. Minimal PoC design (which code, which operator, which figure)

Goal: the smallest experiment that puts a released strong solver, our operator, and our audit on the
**same** measurement, and outputs one table + one figure. Everything except the external solver call
already exists in this repo.

### 3.1 Choice of solver + task (minimize integration cost)
Pick **DDNM (R026)** as the primary target and **DDRM (R070)** as the spectral contrast:

- **DDNM** is the tightest methodological cousin (range fixed to `A†y`, null-only diffusion fill,
  `A x̂ = y` exact), so its outputs land in our exact-range regime with **zero** consistency
  retuning — the cleanest possible "their measured ledger is perfect, their null ledger is
  uncertified" exhibit. Released code: authors' public DDNM repo (`wyhuai/DDNM`), pretrained
  ImageNet/CelebA diffusion checkpoints, MIT-style license.
- **DDRM** adds the `η_b < 1` measured-mode blend, giving a *second*, quantitatively different audit
  signature (measured-mode contraction < 1) on the same figure. Released code: `bahjat-kawar/ddrm`.
- **Operator choice — reuse ours, do not hand-build theirs.** Use the repo's structured GI operator
  (64×64, `total_m = 205`: DCT + Hadamard + random rows) already wired in
  `gan_high_quality_gi.build_structured_operator_rows` / `make_measurement_operator`. It is a valid
  **linear inpainting-style undersampling `A`**, exactly the operator class DDNM/DDRM accept
  (they take arbitrary `A, A†`). This keeps our certificate, our `P_0`, and our fusion on the
  identical operator the locked result uses — removing any "different-operator" caveat.

**Minimal integration contract.** Both DDNM and DDRM are parameterized by `(A, A†)` (DDRM by `SVD(H)`).
We hand them **our** operator's `A_forward` and `A†` (Tikhonov solve with `lambda_dc`, from
`MeasurementProjector`), run their released sampler to produce `x̂_DDNM`, `x̂_DDRM`, then audit.

### 3.2 Audit pipeline (all existing repo code)
For each solver output `x̂`:

1. **Range residual.** `RelMeasErr = ‖A_forward(x̂) − y‖ / ‖y‖` using `MeasurementProjector.A_forward`.
   Expected: DDNM/MCG ≈ 1e-15; DDRM small-but-nonzero (`η_b` cost); DPS/DiffPIR clearly nonzero.
2. **Per-mode certificate.** Report the exact contraction spectrum `λ/(λ+σ_i²)` (P2 operator) on the
   SVD of `A` — a modal spectrum, not one number (differentiates vs Iagaru 2605.13146's bounds).
3. **Feasible-wrong twin.** For each `x̂`, build a **named** twin
   `u = audit_flat(flatten(x_j), y)` with `x_j` a different-class scene
   (reuse `experiments_feasible_wrong.py` verbatim — it already does exactly this on our operator).
   Report `RelMeasErr(u vs y)` (≈1e-15) and `PSNR(u vs true x_i)` (low → visibly wrong).
4. **Governed-fill A/B.** Replace the solver's null fill by our B-dial fusion under the **same** `P_0`
   (`vqgan_detail_fusion` / `experiments_rate_fusion`): show `A x̂_B = y` to ~2e-15 for every `B`,
   next to the solver's own null fill.

Only step (1)–(4) plumbing around the **external** sampler output is new; the audit itself is the
repo's shipped code path.

### 3.4 Scope / honesty caveats (state in the paper)
- The audit certifies the **measured** ledger only; the twin demonstration and the certificate are
  explicitly framed as *what the measurement cannot vouch for*. No claim that any solver — or ours —
  recovers true null content.
- DDRM/DPS/DiffPIR "residuals" are reported as accountability facts, **not** as quality criticisms;
  their PSNR/FID/LPIPS may be excellent. The two ledgers are orthogonal (P2 separation).
- Marked **proposed**: it requires standing up the released DDNM/DDRM checkpoints and samplers and
  feeding them our `A/A†`. No claim is made until that run is executed.

## 4. Why this strengthens P1 + P4 (positioning)
- **P1 (converse).** The proposition upgrades "consistency ≠ correctness" from a theorem about *maps*
  (Gottschling 2025) or an existence result (Iagaru 2605.13146, Thm 3.2/3.6) into a **per-instance,
  per-solver constructive witness at ~1e-15** on the field's *strongest* reconstructors — the deltas
  already logged as uncontested in `NOVELTY_THREAT_DIFFERENTIATION.md` (§52–67).
- **P4 (stance).** It turns the four solvers most likely to be cited *against* us into live exhibits
  *for* us: their strength is exactly what makes "the measurement certifies only the range" vivid.
  The governed-fill A/B (row 6) shows the constructive alternative the assessment-only literature
  lacks. We never claim SOTA and never certify invented texture — the point is that the measurement
  provably cannot.
