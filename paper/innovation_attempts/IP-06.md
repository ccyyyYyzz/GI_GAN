## 6. The null space is the non-identified set: an identifiability reading of the range/null geometry

### 6.1 One object, four disciplines

Our forward model is `y = A x` with `A ∈ ℝ^{m×n}`, `m < n`, so the pseudo-inverse solution decomposes any admissible image as

```
x = A†y  +  P_0 q ,     P_R = A†A ,     P_0 = I − A†A ,     A P_0 = 0 ,     q arbitrary.
```

The literature that shares this geometry (Wang AAAI 2023; RND-SCI; NPN; GSNR; DDNM; DNSL) reads `P_0 q` as *content to be filled* — install the "correct" prior and call the fill a reconstruction. We read the identical `P_0` as the classical **non-identified set**: the family of images that are *observationally equivalent* under the measurement, i.e. `A x_1 = A x_2` (to ~2e-15 on our operator) for every `x_1 − x_2 ∈ range(P_0)`. This is not a new operator; it is a 1950-vintage semantics for the operator everyone already uses.

Under this reading the two halves of our title map onto the two halves of the identification problem, which econometrics separated from estimation seventy years ago:

- **Certify what you measure** = audit the **identified** component `A†y` (the range space `P_R`), the part invariant across all observationally-equivalent structures.
- **Govern what you cannot** = operate the dial entirely inside the **non-identified** set (`P_0`), and label its output invented *by construction*.

The pedigree is deep and cross-disciplinary. The same object — "directions the data cannot distinguish" — carries four names:

**Table 6.1 — The null space renamed four ways (one object, four fields).**

| Field | Source (card) | Name of the object | Formal characterization | Our instance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Econometric identification | Koopmans & Reiersol 1950 (card 83, E001) | **Observationally-equivalent structures** | Structures giving the identical observable distribution `H(y)`; a characteristic is *identifiable* iff invariant across all of them | Images with identical `y`; only `A†y` (range) is invariant, `P_0 q` is not |
| Information-geometry identification | Rothenberg 1971 (card 90/97) | **Singular-information direction** | Local identifiability ⇔ Fisher information `R(α)` nonsingular; a null vector of `R` traces a curve of indistinguishable structures | Null vectors of `A†A`: `P_0 q` directions along which the record is constant |
| Partial identification | Manski 2003 (card 89/96) | **Identification region** (set-valued) | When data + assumptions do not point-identify, the honest object is the *sharp set* of all observationally-consistent values | The feasible-consistent set `{A†y + P_0 q : q}`; picking a `q` picks a point in it |
| Sampling / degrees-of-freedom | Landau 1967 (card 87/94) | **Below-density lost modes** | Below the Landau/Nyquist density the concentration operator's eigenvalues fall from ≈1 to ≈0; DOF exceed measurements | The near-0 SVD tail of `A`: the null space is non-trivial *by necessity*, not by accident |

Two further cards close the loop between the econometric and the imaging sides:

- **Candès–Romberg–Tao 2004** (card 85, E010) supplies the imaging-side converse in the *same* language: their Theorem 1.1 exhibits distinct signals with `|supp| ≤ |Ω|/2 + 1` sharing identical partial-Fourier measurements — a feasible-wrong pair — and their Fig. 1c/1d contrast (min-energy zero-fill `A†y` vs. TV-selected null completion) is Table 6.1 made visible: same range, prior-selected null.
- **Stuart 2010** (card 99) is the probabilistic mirror: when data dimension < unknown, the prior is *not* washed out as noise → 0; the measurement fixes the resolved directions and the prior fixes the unresolved ones. This is Table 6.1's row-space/null-space split stated as a posterior.

### 6.2 The certificate reports the identified row-space; the dial governs the non-identified null-space

The identifiability frame does real work beyond rhetoric: it names, with a rigor bar borrowed from econometrics, *why* our two ledgers are legitimately separate.

**Ledger 1 (measured / identified).** The certificate's per-mode contraction `λ/(λ+σ_i²)` (P2) is exactly the Gaussian-posterior variance reduction along singular mode `i` (Stuart 2010, card 99) — a *soft, per-mode* version of Landau's *hard* 1/0 concentration threshold (card 87). It reports how much of each measured mode the operator accounts for: the identified ledger, invariant across every dial setting `B`.

**Ledger 2 (invented / non-identified).** The dial `B` (P3) chooses *where inside the identification region* the reconstruction lands. Lewbel 2019 (card 84, E004) gives the decisive vocabulary: a restriction is a **free normalization** iff every *meaningful* downstream quantity is invariant to it (the linear-index scale ambiguity `‖β‖ = 1` is the canonical case). Our `B` is a free normalization **with respect to the measurement** — `A x̂_B = y` holds exactly for every `B` — and emphatically **not** with respect to perception: LPIPS moves −32.6% as `B` sweeps. That asymmetry is the honest content of the paper: the dial is free where the data is silent and consequential only where the data was never going to speak.

**The stance (P4), imported from 1950.** Koopmans & Reiersol's own warning — *"scientific honesty demands... not the desire for identifiability"* (card 83) — is the econometric statement of our red line: do not adopt a restriction merely to manufacture an answer the data cannot support. Manski's **Law of Decreasing Credibility** (card 89) is the same principle as a gradient: credibility falls as maintained assumptions strengthen. A learned GI reconstructor that silently hard-codes a null completion is, in Lewbel's terms, relying on **non-robust identification** — a point-ID assumption whose removal loses even set-ID. We instead report the identified set and expose the normalization as a pre-registered, labelled choice.

### 6.3 Positioning against the #1 threat (Iagaru 2026)

Iagaru, Gottschling, Hansen & Garnier 2026 (card 86/93, arXiv:2605.13146) prove the imaging converse — consistent decoders can only hallucinate measurement-invisible detail, `‖f(x+x_det) − f(x)‖ ≤ 2ε` — and summarize null-space ambiguity with a *single scalar*, the worst-case kernel size `diam(F_y)`. The identifiability frame sharpens our delta into one sentence:

> **They report one number for the whole non-identified set; we give its per-mode identifiability spectrum, and then a governed normalization within it.** `diam(F_y)` is a set *diameter* (a global measure of the identification region); our `λ/(λ+σ_i²)` is the *per-direction* identifiability profile (which modes are identified, and how strongly); and our `B` is a pre-registered choice of point *inside* that region with the measurement held exactly. Assessment stops at the diameter; governance acts inside it.

This is fully consistent with the verified threat differentiation: cite Iagaru 2026 (with Gottschling 2025) as the fundamental-limits anchor, claim the **exact modal spectrum**, the **quality/accountability separation**, and the **governed dial** — none of which the assessment-only line contains.

### 6.4 Proposition (identifiability form of the converse) + proof sketch

**Proposition 6.1 (Null content is non-identified).**
*Let `A ∈ ℝ^{m×n}` with `rank(A) = r < n`, and let `x̂_B = A†y + P_0 q(B)` for any dial-selected `q(B) ∈ range(P_0)`. Then:*

1. *(Observational equivalence / P1.)* For every `B, B'`, `A x̂_B = A x̂_{B'} = y`. The map `B ↦ x̂_B` traces a subset of a single observationally-equivalent class in the sense of Koopmans–Reiersol (card 83) and Rothenberg (card 90): the record is constant along all `range(P_0)` directions, which are exactly the singular/null directions of the information operator `A†A`.

2. *(Free normalization / P3, P4.)* The choice of `B` is a free normalization *with respect to the measurement functional* `x ↦ Ax` (Lewbel, card 84): the identified quantity `A x̂_B = y` is invariant to `B`. It is *not* free with respect to any functional that reads `range(P_0)` (e.g. a perceptual metric), so no measurement-derived certificate can adjudicate `B`.

3. *(Non-emptiness by necessity / classical anchor.)* Whenever `A` is below the Landau/Nyquist density (card 87) — the operating regime of ghost imaging, `m ≪ n` — `range(P_0)` is non-trivial by necessity, so a genuine non-identified set always exists to govern.

**Proof sketch.**
(1) `A P_0 = 0` gives `A x̂_B = A A†y = y` for all `B`; the difference of any two dial states lies in `range(P_0) = ker(A)`, so both map to the same `y`. Identifying `A†A` with a (degenerate) information operator, its zero eigen-directions are Rothenberg's singular-information directions and Koopmans–Reiersol's observational-equivalence directions — hence "same record, different image" is literal non-identification, not approximation. Empirically the equivalence holds to `‖A x̂_B − y‖ ≈ 2e-15` on the fixed operator (P1 witness), and Candès–Romberg–Tao's Theorem 1.1 (card 85) is the classical exact-arithmetic instance of the same statement.
(2) By definition `A x̂_B = y` is constant in `B`; take any linear functional `ℓ` with `ℓ P_0 ≠ 0` (a perceptual read of null content) and it varies with `B`. So `B` satisfies Lewbel's free-normalization test against the measurement and fails it against perception — precisely the ledger split.
(3) `dim range(P_0) = n − r ≥ n − m > 0`; Landau's density theorem makes this a *necessity* below Nyquist rather than an artifact of a particular `A`. ∎

**Corollary 6.2 (why a measurement certificate cannot rank dial settings).** Any certificate that is a function of `(A, y)` alone is constant across `{x̂_B}` because `(A, y)` is. Therefore no such certificate can prefer one `B`; the dial must be *governed and labelled*, not *certified*. This is the identifiability restatement of the honesty red line.

### 6.5 Experiment-design proposal — the identifiability panel (GI-native, from existing assets)

A single four-panel figure operationalizes Table 6.1 on a ghost-imaging operator already in the repo. No ground truth is used to produce any certificate value.

- **Panel A — Non-identification witness (P1).** Two feasible-wrong twins `x̂` and `x̂ + P_0 v` on the fixed GI operator; report `‖A x̂ − y‖` and `‖A(x̂ + P_0 v) − y‖`, both ≈ 2e-15, with visibly different null content. Caption: *Koopmans–Reiersol observational equivalence, realized at machine precision.* (Reuses the P1 null-constrained perturbation; grounded in cards 83, 90, 85.)
- **Panel B — Identifiability spectrum (P2).** Plot `λ/(λ+σ_i²)` across singular modes (the identified profile) beside a single horizontal line at Iagaru's `diam(F_y)`-style scalar. Caption: *We report which directions are identified; the assessment-only bound reports one diameter of the non-identified set.* (Cards 86, 99, 87.)
- **Panel C — Free-normalization check (P3/P4).** Sweep `B`; overlay `‖A x̂_B − y‖` (flat at 2e-15 → free normalization w.r.t. measurement) and LPIPS (−32.6% → not free w.r.t. perception). Caption: *`B` is Lewbel-free for the data, decisive for perception.* (Card 84.)
- **Panel D — Identification region cartoon.** Schematic of the affine set `A†y + range(P_0)` with the dial trajectory `{x̂_B}` drawn inside it and the identified point `A†y` marked. Caption: *Manski's identification region; the dial is a governed choice within the set the data cannot narrow.* (Card 89.)

**Falsifiable prediction (adopting Iagaru's consistency-falsifiability framing):** every point on the Panel-C dial trajectory must satisfy the Panel-A residual bound; any `B` that lowers the residual below the range-space floor would falsify the claim that the dial edits only `range(P_0)`. This makes the whole subsection testable on the operator, not merely asserted.

### 6.6 What this buys the paper

1. **Gravitas / depth reviewers reward.** It converts `P_0` from "a CS trick" into a genuine cross-disciplinary object with a 1950 pedigree (Koopmans–Reiersol → Rothenberg → Manski → Lewbel; Landau; Candès–Romberg–Tao; Stuart), answering "why should imaging people care about identifiability?" — *because certifying a reconstruction is exactly deciding which directions are identified.*
2. **Differentiation from the #1 threat**, restated crisply as *per-mode identifiability spectrum + governed normalization* vs. *a single kernel-size diameter* (§6.3).
3. **A rigor bar for the dial** (Lewbel's free-normalization test) and an **honesty principle with authority** (Koopmans' warning; Manski's Law of Decreasing Credibility) — both directly serving P4.
4. **Honesty red line held throughout:** the certificate audits the identified ledger; the dial governs — and labels invented — the non-identified ledger; Corollary 6.2 proves the measurement *cannot* certify the dial, so no such claim is ever made.
