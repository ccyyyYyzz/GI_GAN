# IP-08 — Two-Ledgers Instrument: exact measurement certificate ⊕ statistical task guarantee

**Realized artifact.** A paper-ready subsection (proposition + proof sketch + worked recipe + experiment
design + related-work positioning) specifying how our **exact, per-image, ground-truth-free measurement
certificate** composes with a **distribution-free statistical guarantee on a downstream task**, without ever
conflating the two. Two orthogonal ledgers, two guarantee *types*, one honest joint statement.

**Status:** `proposed` — the compositional statement and proof sketch are complete and self-contained; the

  our certificate. Its size-stratified-risk diagnostic ("sets must not systematically fail on the hardest
  pixels") is the analogue we invoke for the hardest (high-frequency) null modes.

  redirect UQ from per-pixel maps to the **downstream task scalar** `f(x̂)`; per-pixel maps miss many-pixel
  structural hallucination. Supplies the *task-output* object our second ledger controls, and the
  "collect measurements until task-uncertainty < η" acquisition protocol we cite as the achievability companion.

**Honesty red line (held).** The measurement certificate audits only the *row-space* ledger; it never
certifies null-space content. The task-risk ledger is a *statistical, label-based* guarantee about a
downstream functional — it likewise does **not** certify that any injected texture is the true scene. Neither
ledger, alone or composed, converts invented detail into fact. The composition is designed precisely to make
that separation legible: the two guarantees are reported in different currencies and are never summed.

## §X. Two Ledgers: an Exact Measurement Certificate and a Statistical Task Guarantee

### X.1 Why two ledgers, and why they must not be conflated

The uncertainty-quantification literature for inverse problems offers a menu of guarantees, but they are all
of one *kind*: statistical and calibration-dependent. Risk-controlling prediction sets endow any image
regressor with per-pixel intervals that cover a chosen fraction of ground-truth pixels with high probability,
purchased with an i.i.d. calibration set (Angelopoulos et al., 2022). Task-driven conformal prediction
redirects the guarantee from per-pixel maps to a downstream scalar `f(x̂)` — a classifier's tear score,
a lesion count — arguing that per-pixel maps are blind to many-pixel hallucinated structure (Wen et al.,
2024). Conformal risk control generalizes both, controlling `E[L] ≤ α` for any bounded monotone loss with a
distribution-free finite-sample guarantee (Angelopoulos et al., 2024). Bayesian UQ adds an epistemic/aleatoric
split (Kendall & Gal, 2017; Narnhofer et al., 2021). Every one of these guarantees is **statistical**: it
holds *on average over a distribution*, it needs *labels* to calibrate, and it makes a claim about how far a
reconstruction may sit from a ground truth drawn from that distribution.

Our certificate (§4) is a different object with a different guarantee type. It is **exact** (a closed-form
per-mode contraction `c_i(λ) = λ/(λ + σ_i²)`, not a coverage probability), **per-image** (it holds for the
single record `y` in front of us, not on average over a test distribution), and **ground-truth-free** (it
needs only `A`, `y`, and `λ`; no calibration set, no i.i.d. assumption). It certifies exactly one thing —
that a reconstruction reproduces the measured row-space component — and, by the barrier of §3, it is *silent*
on the null space by construction.

These are not competing answers to one question; they answer two different questions, and the central
contribution of this section is to **keep them apart while letting them cooperate.** We call them the
**measurement ledger** (ours: exact, per-image, label-free, silent on the null space) and the **task ledger**
(conformal: statistical, on-average, label-based, about a downstream functional). The honest instrument reports
both, in their own currencies, and never sums them.

> **The one-line firewall.** The measurement ledger certifies *what the bucket accounts for*. The task ledger
> bounds *how much a chosen downstream decision may suffer* as we inject unmeasured detail. Neither certifies
> that the injected detail is real.

### X.2 The compositional statement

Fix the operator `A` (`m < n`), a record `y = Ax + ε`, and the governed family of §6,
`x̂_B = x_0 + P_0(d_A + B(d_G − d_A))`, with audited anchor `A x_0 = y`. Let `f : ℝⁿ → ℝ` be a downstream
task functional (e.g. a detector's score), and let `L_B = ℓ(f(x̂_B), f(x))` be a per-example task loss that is
**bounded** (`0 ≤ L ≤ L_max`) and **monotone non-decreasing in B** — i.e. injecting more adversarial detail
never *decreases* the risk that the task decision is corrupted. (Monotonicity is a design obligation on the
loss, discussed in X.5; it is exactly the structural hypothesis conformal risk control requires.)

**Proposition (Two Ledgers).** *For the governed family `x̂_B`:*

1. **(Measurement ledger — exact, per-image, label-free.)** *For every `B ∈ ℝ` and the single record `y`, the
   fused reconstruction satisfies `A x̂_B = y` exactly (§6.3), and the plug-in audit `Π_y^λ` contracts each
   measured mode of any reconstruction's residual by exactly `c_i(λ) = λ/(λ + σ_i²)` (§4.2). This guarantee
   uses no ground truth and no calibration data; it holds deterministically for this image.*

2. **(Task ledger — statistical, distribution-free, label-based.)** *Given a held-out calibration set
   `{(y_k, x_k)}_{k=1}^{K}` drawn exchangeably with the test instance and the monotone bounded loss `L_B`,
   conformal risk control selects*
   > `B̂ = inf { B : (K/(K+1)) R̂_K(B) + L_max/(K+1) ≤ α }`,  where `R̂_K(B) = (1/K) Σ_k L_B(y_k, x_k)`,

   *the largest admissible detail level whose empirical task risk (inflated by the finite-sample slack) stays
   under `α`. Then the selected operating point satisfies the distribution-free, finite-sample guarantee*
   > `E[ L_{B̂}(y_{K+1}, x_{K+1}) ] ≤ α`,

   *tight to `O(1/K)` (Angelopoulos et al., 2024, Thms 1–2).*

3. **(Orthogonality / non-conflation.)** *The two guarantees are logically independent. Statement 1 holds for
   `B̂` and for every other `B` regardless of the calibration outcome; statement 2 constrains only the task
   functional and is silent on `A x̂ = y`. Composing them yields the honest joint claim*
   > *"the reconstruction `x̂_{B̂}` reproduces the measurement exactly (`A x̂_{B̂} = y`, certified per-image
   > and ground-truth-free), **and** the injected detail is throttled to a level at which a pre-specified
   > downstream task incurs expected loss at most `α` (certified statistically on a labeled calibration set)."*
   *Neither clause certifies that `P_0 x̂_{B̂}` is the true null-space content.*

Because the two ledgers touch **different subspaces of the guarantee space** — the measurement ledger lives
entirely in the row space (`A P_0 = 0` makes it blind to `B`) while the task ledger lives entirely in the
task-output response to null-space detail (`B` moves only `P_0`) — the composition is *conflict-free by
construction*: raising or lowering `B̂` never disturbs statement 1, and statement 1 never constrains `B̂`.
This is the same `A P_0 = 0` geometry that drives the converse (§3) and the exact-consistency dial (§6),
now separating the two guarantee *types* rather than two images.

### X.3 Proof sketch

*Statement 1* is §6.3 (consistency theorem: `A x̂_B = A x_0 + A P_0(·) = y + 0 = y` for all `B`) and §4.2
(mode contraction, verified in float64 to `1.04×10⁻¹⁰` on Rad-5). Nothing statistical enters; it is a
consequence of the SVD of `A` and holds for the single instance.

*Statement 2* is a direct instantiation of conformal risk control (Angelopoulos et al., 2024). The family
`{x̂_B}` is nested in the required sense: `B` is a scalar conservativeness knob and `L_B` is, by the design
hypothesis of X.5, non-increasing in the *conservative direction* (small `B` = little injected detail = low
task-corruption risk), which is the monotonicity CRC needs. Boundedness `L ≤ L_max` is imposed by clipping the
loss. CRC's Theorem 1 then gives `E[L_{B̂}] ≤ α` for exchangeable calibration data, and Theorem 2 gives the
matching lower bound `α − 2 L_max/(K+1)`, so the control is tight, not merely conservative. The only inputs
beyond the pre-trained dial are the labeled calibration pairs and the choice of `f`, `ℓ`, `α` — all declared
before the confirmatory split, consistent with the program's pre-registration discipline.

*Statement 3* follows because the two selection procedures act on orthogonal quantities. `B̂` is chosen by the
calibration loss on `f`; statement 1's residual `A x̂_B − y` is identically zero for every `B` (§6.3), so the
measurement ledger is invariant to the calibration outcome. Conversely, the audit `Π_y^λ` touches only the row
space (`P_0 Π_y^λ(v) = P_0 v`, §4.1), so it cannot change `f`'s dependence on the null-space detail. The
guarantees therefore neither reinforce nor undermine each other; they are *reported side by side in different
units* (a per-image residual at machine precision; an expected task loss `≤ α` at confidence set by `K`).

### X.4 Worked recipe (buildable on the existing B-dial)

The recipe adds a calibration layer on top of the frozen fusion assets of §6–§7; it changes no reconstruction
code and touches no confirmatory image.

1. **Declare the task.** Pick a downstream functional `f` on the reconstruction — e.g. a fixed
   binary/one-vs-rest detector on the STL10 semantic class, or a segmentation-derived count — and a bounded
   loss `ℓ`, e.g. **detection FNR** (false-negative rate), the same loss CRC controls in its polyp benchmark
   (R081). Fix risk level `α` (say `0.1`) and confidence via calibration size `K`. Declare `f`, `ℓ`, `α`, `K`
   **before** unlocking the confirmatory split (mirrors the §7 locked-gate discipline).
2. **Build the loss curve on calibration data.** For a labeled calibration set `{(y_k, x_k)}` disjoint from
   the confirmatory split, sweep `B` over the pre-registered grid and record `R̂_K(B)`, the mean task loss.
   Verify empirically that `R̂_K(B)` is monotone in the conservative direction (X.5); if it is not, redefine
   the direction or the loss so it is (CRC requires monotonicity, not that reality be monotone).
3. **Calibrate `B̂`.** Apply the CRC selection
   `B̂ = inf{ B : (K/(K+1)) R̂_K(B) + L_max/(K+1) ≤ α }`. This returns the *largest* detail level whose task
   risk provably stays under `α` — the honest ceiling on how much invented detail a downstream user may admit
   before the task guarantee lapses.
4. **Report both ledgers, side by side, in different currencies.**
   - *Measurement ledger (ours):* `RelMeasErr(x̂_{B̂}) ∼ 10⁻⁷` (round-off; §6.4), and the per-mode certificate
     spectrum `c_i(λ) = λ/(λ + σ_i²)` — exact, per-image, ground-truth-free. This is **flat across the entire
     `B` sweep**, exactly the §5 separation.
   - *Task ledger (conformal):* `E[FNR(x̂_{B̂})] ≤ α`, distribution-free, tight to `O(1/K)`, valid only on the
     calibration distribution and only for the declared task.
5. **State the joint claim in the paper's own honesty language.** "At the calibrated operating point `B̂`, the
   reconstruction reproduces the buckets exactly and the injected detail is throttled so the declared detector's
   expected FNR is at most `α`; the measurement still certifies nothing about the null-space texture — that
   remains a governed, labeled-as-invented hypothesis."

**The money figure.** One panel, two curves against `B` on a shared x-axis:
- the **measurement residual** `‖A x̂_B − y‖ / ‖y‖`, pinned flat at `∼10⁻⁷` for every `B`
  (already produced by the §6.4 run — the residual is round-off at all operating points);
- the **task risk** `R̂_K(B)` rising with `B`, with the CRC-selected `B̂` and the level `α` marked, and the
  per-mode certificate spectrum inset as flat.

The reader sees at a glance: **the measurement ledger does not move as detail is injected (it cannot —
`A P_0 = 0`); only the task ledger moves, and it is the task ledger — never the measurement — that decides how
much detail is admissible.** This is the two-ledgers thesis in one image, and it is the visual dual of the §5
separation law (there: quality vs accountability; here: task-risk vs measurement-accountability).

### X.5 The monotonicity obligation (and why it is honest, not a cheat)

CRC requires the controlled loss to be monotone in the knob. This is a genuine design obligation, and naming it
is part of the honesty. The natural expectation — more adversarial detail can only *add* ways for a downstream
detector to be misled about content the measurement never constrained — makes FNR-style losses plausibly
monotone non-decreasing in `B`, but this is an empirical property to *verify* on calibration data (step 2),
not assume. Two guards: (i) if `R̂_K(B)` is non-monotone, replace `L_B` by its running maximum
`L̃_B = max_{B' ≤ B} L_{B'}` (a valid bounded monotone surrogate that only *tightens* the guarantee), as CRC
permits for non-monotone base losses; (ii) report the raw `R̂_K(B)` curve so the reader sees the true shape.
This mirrors the size-stratified-risk diagnostic of R076 (a guarantee well-calibrated *on average* can still
fail systematically on the hardest cases): here the hardest cases are the high-frequency null modes where
VQGAN detail is most aggressive, and the raw curve exposes whether the task risk concentrates there.

### X.6 Positioning (related work)

| | Measurement ledger (**ours**, §4) | Task ledger (conformal, this section, from cards) |
|---|---|---|
| Guarantee type | **exact** closed-form contraction `λ/(λ+σ_i²)` | **statistical** `E[L] ≤ α`, tight to `O(1/K)` |
| Scope | **per-image**, this record `y` | **on-average** over calibration distribution |
| Data need | **none** (ground-truth-free) | **labeled** calibration set (i.i.d./exchangeable) |
| Object certified | measured **row-space** component | downstream **task functional** `f(x̂)` |
| Null space | provably **silent** (`A P_0 = 0`) | indirectly, via `f`'s response to injected detail |
| Source | this paper | Angelopoulos 2022/2024 (R076, R081); Wen 2024 (R077) |

The conformal/UQ cluster (R076 RCPS, R077 task-driven CP, R078 ordinal CP, R081 CRC) is the leading family of
*test-time guarantees on reconstructions*, and it is the right companion — not competitor — to our certificate:
it answers the **statistical task** question our exact certificate deliberately does not, while our certificate
answers the **exact, label-free, per-image measurement** question no calibration set can (the null content is
provably unverifiable, §3, so no amount of labeled data certifies it). Against the Bayesian branch (R079, R080)
we add that accountability is a **third axis** orthogonal to aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty: it neither
shrinks with data nor is observation noise; it is the operator's identification deficiency. The two-ledgers
instrument is thus the constructive synthesis the UQ literature gestures at (R081's own `innovation_spark`) but
does not build, because it lacks the exact measurement certificate to place on the other side of the ledger.

### X.7 What this does *not* claim (red-line restatement)

- It does **not** claim the measurement certifies any injected texture. Statement 1 certifies `A x̂_B = y`;
  §3 proves `P_0 x̂_B` is unverifiable from `y`. The task ledger does not repair this — a task guarantee on
  `f(x̂)` is a claim about a *decision*, not about the truth of the pixels feeding it.
- It does **not** claim the composed guarantee is stronger than either part; it is exactly the conjunction of
  a per-image measurement fact and a distribution-level task bound, reported in separate currencies.
- It does **not** claim SOTA task performance, nor that `B̂` is optimal for reconstruction. `B̂` is the honest
  *ceiling on admissible invention* for a declared task at a declared risk level — a governance quantity, not
  a quality one.
