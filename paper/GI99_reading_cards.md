## 1. GAN Prior Based Null-Space Learning for Consistent Super-resolution

*AAAI 2023 (Wang, Hu, Yu, Zhang; Peking Univ.)* — `R001_GAN_Prior_Based_Null_Space_Learning_for_Consistent_Super_resolution.pdf`

- **What:** Enforces exact measurement consistency in GAN-prior super-resolution by fixing the range-space part to A^dagger y and letting the network populate ONLY the null-space, via a parameter-free pooling-based decomposition (PD).
- **Method:** Uses the range-null decomposition x = A^dagger A x + (I - A^dagger A)x, and constructs the SR output as x_hat = A^dagger y + (I - A^dagger A) x_r, where x_r is the raw GAN-prior network prediction. This guarantees A x_hat = y exactly (consistency) while realness comes from the GAN prior. Because a general downsampler's pseudo-inverse is hard to obtain, they instantiate A as average-pooling whose pseudo-inverse P_up is pixel replication, giving the closed form x_hat = P_up(y) + x_r - P_up(P_down(x_r)); the network learns only high-frequency null-space content. PD is parameter-free, adds negligible compute, and can be dropped into existing GAN-prior SR backbones (Panini, GLEAN).
- **Results:** With PD, downsampling-consistency PSNR(y, P_down(x_hat)) = 145.7 dB (i.e., A x_hat = y holds to numerical precision), vs only 42.2 dB for the FFT-based CEM baseline whose pseudo-inverse is lossy. PD raises reconstruction: 8x face SR PSNR 27.13->29.97 (Panini) and 27.20->30.21 (GLEAN); accelerates convergence 2-10x (10K iters match 90K without PD); improves generalization to unseen downsamplings (FID 30.47->14.11) and real-world degradation without ever using pixel-wise losses (adversarial-only PDN variant).
- **Relevance:** P3 (governed dial) + P2 background. This is the closest architectural predecessor to our null-space injection dial: same A x_hat = y-by-construction guarantee via A^dagger y + (I-A^dagger A)(.). Cite in Responsible-Supply(ACT2)/Dial section as prior art that puts generated content in the null space, and POSITION AGAINST it in Geometry/HINGE: they treat consistency-by-construction as a virtue (correctness proxy) whereas our converse (P1) shows exactly this construction is why measurement consistency CANNOT certify the invented null content. Also cite in Related-Work as the origin of the pooling pseudo-inverse trick.
- **Cite:** Wang et al. (AAAI 2023) show that fixing the range-space to A^dagger y and letting a GAN prior fill only the null-space yields super-resolution outputs that satisfy A x_hat = y to ~145 dB by construction, decoupling measurement consistency from the invented high-frequency content.
- **💡 Spark:** Their 145.7 dB consistency-PSNR number is a concrete empirical anchor for our P1 claim that feasible-wrong images satisfy the same measurement to ~2e-15 -- we can cite it as independent confirmation that null-space edits are measurement-invisible, then flip their framing: they celebrate it as 'consistency', we expose it as the verifiability barrier. Their adversarial-only PDN (no pixel loss, better real-world generalization) also motivates our B-dial argument that null-space content is a governance choice, not a fidelity target.

## 2. HalluGen: Synthesizing Realistic and Controllable Hallucinations for Evaluating Image Restoration

*arXiv 2512.03345 (Dec 2025), cs.CV; Kim et al., UCL/AstraZeneca* — `R002_HalluGen_Synthesizing_Realistic_and_Controllable_Hallucinations_for_Evaluating_Image_Restoration.pdf`

- **What:** A diffusion-based generator that synthesizes controllable, labeled hallucinations for restoration, grounded in a measurement-consistency taxonomy: intrinsic hallucinations violate A(x_hat)!=A(x_gt), extrinsic ones stay consistent A(x_hat)=A(x_gt) but differ in the inverse (null) domain.
- **Method:** Formalizes hallucinations via the forward model y = A(x) + n. Defines INTRINSIC hallucinations as those violating measurement consistency (A(x_hat) != A(x_gt)) and EXTRINSIC as measurement-consistent but semantically wrong (A(x_hat) = A(x_gt) but A^{-1}(A(x_hat)) != A^{-1}(A(x_gt))). Builds on Diffusion Posterior Sampling (DPS): to create intrinsic hallucinations it does gradient ASCENT on the data-consistency term inside a masked patch and descent outside; for extrinsic ones (analogous to a null space, which for nonlinear A cannot be computed explicitly) it adds pixel- and feature-space (DINO/SAM/MedSAM) divergence terms while keeping measurement fidelity. Also proposes SHAFE, a soft-attention feature metric to detect hallucinations, and a reference-free detector.
- **Results:** Standard metrics are measurement-blind to extrinsic hallucinations: PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS assign HIGHER scores to hallucinated outputs than to slightly blurred-but-correct ones (e.g., hallucinated PSNR 29.15 > blurred 23.68). Pixel metrics detect intrinsic (consistency-violating) hallucinations moderately but degrade sharply on extrinsic (consistency-preserving) ones. Builds first large-scale labeled set: 4,350 annotated images from 1,450 brain MRIs; segmentation IoU drops 0.86->0.36 under hallucination; SHAFE improves detection AUC by 0.25 and cuts false negatives 24 pp; expert inter-annotator agreement is only kappa=0.30, motivating synthetic labels.
- **Relevance:** P1 (converse) + P2 (certificate) + Verifiability-Barrier(HINGE) -- MUST-CITE. Their intrinsic/extrinsic split is an independent, contemporaneous articulation of exactly our range/null dichotomy: extrinsic = null-space = measurement-consistent-but-wrong = provably unverifiable from y alone. Cite in Intro/HINGE to establish the problem is recognized and safety-critical, and position our contribution as the GEOMETRIC/CERTIFICATE upgrade: for LINEAR A the extrinsic (null) space IS computable exactly (P_0 = I - A^dagger A), so our certificate can quantify accountability per mode rather than needing a synthetic benchmark + learned detector.
- **Cite:** Kim et al. (2025) taxonomize restoration hallucinations into intrinsic (violating A(x_hat)=A(x_gt)) and extrinsic (measurement-consistent but semantically wrong), and show PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS systematically score consistent hallucinations higher than correct-but-blurred images.
- **💡 Spark:** Their key admission -- 'for general (nonlinear) operators such regions, analogous to a null space, cannot be computed explicitly' -- is the exact gap our paper fills for LINEAR undersampled A, where P_0 = I - A^dagger A is exact and closed-form. ## 3. GSNR: Graph-Smooth Null-Space Representation for Inverse Problems

*arXiv 2602.20328 v2 (Apr 2026), cs.CV; Gualdron-Hurtado, Jacome, Suarez, Arguello (UIS Colombia)* — `R003_GSNR_Graph_Smooth_Null_Space_Representation_for_Inverse_Problems.pdf`

- **What:** Imposes structure ONLY on the null-space component by building a low-dimensional basis from the p smoothest graph-Laplacian modes restricted to Null(H), with provable minimax coverage and predictability bounds for the invisible directions.
- **Method:** Uses RNSD x = x_r + x_n with P_r = H^dagger H, P_n = I - H^dagger H. Given a graph Laplacian L (4/8-NN pixel grid), forms the null-restricted Laplacian T = P_n L P_n and selects S as the p smoothest eigenmodes (lowest graph frequencies) of T as a projection basis for Null(H). Adds an optional null-only graph regularizer at inference. Two optimality criteria are formalized: COVERAGE (how much null-space variance p modes capture; Theorem 1 shows Laplacian spectra beat the flat spectrum of the bare projector P_n) and PREDICTABILITY (how well Sx can be linearly estimated from y; Proposition 1 gives per-mode R^2 rho_j^2 <= c_j/(c_j+mu_j) under a Gaussian prior). Theorem 2 proves the smoothest T-modes are minimax-optimal for null-space coverage.
- **Results:** Plugs into PnP, DIP, and diffusion solvers across deblurring, CS, demosaicing, SR. Reports up to +4.3 dB PSNR over baseline formulations and up to +1 dB over end-to-end learned models. Shows geometry-free (L=I, i.e., bare P_n) designs give only linear coverage growth (C_I(p)=p/q) and no minimax improvement (mu_{p+1}=1), whereas graph Laplacians achieve faster coverage and stronger statistical coupling to the measurements.
- **Relevance:** P3 (governed dial) + Geometry/Related-Work -- POSITION-AGAINST / must-cite. Same P_0 = I - A^dagger A starting point and same philosophy of governing content in the null-space, but their goal is to REDUCE hallucination/bias by choosing 'good' null directions, treating the null-space fill as something to optimize for fidelity. Our stance (P4) is the opposite governance claim: no choice of null basis is certifiable from y, so we should DIAL it explicitly and never claim the measurement certifies it. Cite their coverage/predictability formalism as the state of the art in structured null-space priors and contrast with our impossibility-first framing.
- **Cite:** Gualdron-Hurtado et al. (2026) construct a graph-smooth basis for Null(H) via the null-restricted Laplacian T = P_n L P_n, proving its smoothest modes are minimax-optimal for null-space coverage and gain up to 4.3 dB by structuring only the sensor-invisible component.
- **💡 Spark:** Their PREDICTABILITY bound rho_j^2 <= c_j/(c_j+mu_j) -- how well a null-space coefficient can be estimated from y -- is a beautiful quantitative counterpart to our certificate's per-mode contraction lambda/(lambda+sigma_i^2). Both are per-mode 'accountability' fractions. We could explicitly connect: their predictability measures how much of the null a prior can *guess*, while our contraction measures how much a measured mode is *audited*; together they draw a clean line between guessed-null (unverifiable) and measured-range (certifiable). Their Theorem 1 flat-spectrum result (bare P_n gives only linear coverage) is also a nice technical hook for why unstructured null-space is genuinely high-dimensional and thus unverifiable.

## 4. RND-SCI: A Range-Null Space Decomposition Approach for Fast and Flexible Spectral Compressive Imaging

*arXiv 2305.09746 (May 2023), cs.CV; Wang et al., HUST* — `R004_A_Range_Null_Space_Decomposition_Approach_for_Fast_and_Flexible_Spectral_Compressive_Imaging.pdf`

- **What:** Applies the range-null decomposition to CASSI hyperspectral snapshot reconstruction: range-space term x_r = Phi^dagger y is computed in closed form for data consistency, and a conditional network generates only the null-space term x_n = (I - Phi^dagger Phi)q.
- **Method:** For the CASSI forward model y = Phi x with sensing matrix Phi (diagonal shifted coded-aperture structure), uses the general solution x_hat = Phi^dagger y + (I - Phi^dagger Phi) q, where Phi Phi^dagger Phi = Phi (range projector) and Phi(I - Phi^dagger Phi) = 0 (null projector). The data-fidelity/range term is a pure linear projection needing no learning; a conditional generative network produces q from (Y, Phi) and is mapped into the null-space. Derives a simple CASSI-specific projection operation and builds it into an enhanced SAUNet (RND-SAUNet). Framework supports training-from-scratch, fine-tuning, and plug-and-play use on pretrained models.
- **Results:** RND-SCI consistently improves 4 different HSI reconstruction networks when retrained, fine-tuned, or plugged in, with minimal overhead. RND-SAUNet reaches 91 frames/s on a Tesla V100 (about 2x the speed of CST-S) while maintaining or improving reconstruction accuracy, giving a strong speed-accuracy tradeoff among real-time HSI methods.
- **Relevance:** P3 (governed dial) + Related-Work background. Another concrete instantiation of the identical A^dagger y + (I - A^dagger A)(.) construction, but in a DIFFERENT modality (hyperspectral compressive/coded-aperture imaging) -- valuable for showing our geometry generalizes beyond faces/MRI to spectral CS, adjacent to ghost/single-pixel imaging. - **Cite:** Wang et al. (2023) reconstruct hyperspectral snapshots as x_hat = Phi^dagger y + (I - Phi^dagger Phi)q, computing the data-consistent range term without learning and letting a conditional network hallucinate only the null-space term, at 91 fps.
- **💡 Spark:** RND-SCI extends the exact same range-null construction to coded-aperture spectral CS, which is operationally close to single-pixel/ghost imaging (structured sensing matrix, m<<n). ## 5. NPN: Non-Linear Projections of the Null-Space for Imaging Inverse Problems

*NeurIPS 2025 (arXiv 2510.01608 v2, Oct 2025); Jacome, Gualdron-Hurtado, Suarez, Arguello (UIS Colombia)* — `R005_NPN_Non_Linear_Projections_of_the_Null_Space_for_Imaging_Inverse_Problems.pdf`

- **What:** A regularizer that promotes solutions lying in a learned low-dimensional projection S of the sensing null-space, training a neural net to predict null-space coefficients Sx* from measurements y, with PnP convergence guarantees.
- **Method:** Starting from RNSD x = P_r(x) + P_n(x) with P_r = H^dagger H, designs a projection matrix S (with rows in Null(H) via orthogonalization or analytic construction, dimension p <= n-m) and trains a network G(y) to predict the null-space coefficients Sx*. Reconstruction penalizes ||G(y) - S x_tilde||^2, concentrating regularization on the most informative unobserved directions rather than the whole image manifold. S and G are jointly optimized. Provides theoretical guarantees: a convergence-improvement zone (CIZ) for PnP whose size is controlled by the network's estimation error, and shows the NPN regularizer is minimized up to a constant depending on that error at the optimum. Complementary to conventional image-domain priors.
- **Results:** In a R^3 toy example, the learned G*(y) closely matches the true null-space projection Sx* on both train and OOD test points, while projecting a direct reconstruction into S gives large errors; OOD error grows only mildly for NPN vs drastic amplification for direct reconstruction. Consistently improves reconstruction fidelity across five inverse problems (CS, MRI, deblurring, SR, CT) with PnP, unrolling networks, DIP, and diffusion solvers.
- **Relevance:** P3 (governed dial) + Geometry/Related-Work -- must-cite, position-against. NPN is the direct methodological parent of GSNR (R003) and the most explicit 'learn structure in the null-space' method; its central premise is that data fidelity 'does not sufficiently constrain the null-space components' and any denoiser can arbitrarily modify null content -- which is precisely our P1 converse, but they treat it as a nuisance to fix with a learned prior. Cite as the leading learned-null-space regularizer and contrast: NPN tries to make the null predictable; our thesis is that null content is fundamentally unverifiable from y and should be GOVERNED (dial B) and never certified.
- **Cite:** Jacome et al. (NeurIPS 2025) note that data fidelity leaves the null-space of H uncontrolled -- 'any component in the null-space may be arbitrarily modified by the denoiser' -- and train a network to predict a low-dimensional null-space projection Sx* from measurements.
- **💡 Spark:** NPN's framing sentence -- data fidelity 'leaves the null-space of H uncontrolled: any component in the null-space of H may be arbitrarily modified by the denoiser' -- is essentially a plain-language statement of our converse (P1) written by null-space-prior advocates, which we can quote to show even proponents concede consistency does not pin down the null. ## 6. Optical imaging by means of two-photon quantum entanglement

*Physical Review A 52, R3429 (1995), Rapid Communications* — `R007_Optical_imaging_by_means_of_two_photon_quantum_entanglement.pdf`

- **What:** The founding 'ghost imaging' experiment: a magnified image of an aperture appears in the coincidence-counting rate of a photon whose beam never touched the aperture, using SPDC entangled signal-idler pairs.
- **Method:** Pittman, Shih, Strekalov and Sergienko pump a BBO crystal (351.1 nm argon laser) to generate type-II SPDC signal/idler photon pairs entangled in transverse momentum (state |Psi> = sum delta(w_s+w_i-w_p) delta(k_s+k_i-k_p)|k_s>|k_i>). The signal beam passes a convex lens and an aperture (letters 'UMBC') to a fixed bucket detector D1; the idler beam is scanned transversely by a fiber tip (D2). No image is visible in either detector's singles rate, but the coincidence rate as a function of idler-fiber position reproduces a sharp, magnified image of the aperture. A 'two-photon Gaussian thin lens equation' 1/S+1/S'=1/f (with S=600mm, S'=1200mm) governs the object-image geometry via an 'unfolded' hinge-point picture where equal-and-opposite emission angles map object points to image points.
- **Results:** Magnification factor of 2 (aperture ~3.5x7 mm imaged to 7x14 mm); image formed purely in coincidences while singles rates stay constant; scanning step 0.25 mm; no image enhancement applied. Establishes point-by-point object-image correspondence defined only through the two-photon correlation, not through either arm alone.
- **Relevance:** P1 (Converse) + Related-Work + Intro. This is the historical origin of the entire ghost-imaging modality that our paper generalizes; must-cite as the seminal GI reference that motivates the physical instantiation of our undersampled operator A. The fact that object information exists ONLY in the joint two-photon correlation (invisible in any single-arm marginal) is a physical precursor to our geometric point that measured (range) content and unmeasured content are structurally distinct.
- **Cite:** The first ghost-imaging experiment (Pittman et al., 1995) recovered a magnified aperture image purely from photon-coincidence correlations, with no image present in either detector's individual counts.
- **💡 Spark:** Frame ghost imaging historically as a modality where 'what you can measure' (the correlation) was always physically separated from 'what any single arm sees' — a physical antecedent to our range/null split. Cite it in the Intro to ground A x = y in a real optical measurement chain, then note our contribution is making the unmeasured-content boundary explicit and certifiable rather than merely reconstructing it.

## 7. Correlated imaging, quantum and classical (Gatti, Brambilla, Bache, Lugiato)

*arXiv:quant-ph/0307187 (2003); published Phys. Rev. A 70, 013802 / PRL 93, 093602 (2004)* — `R008_Ghost_Imaging_with_Thermal_Light_Comparing_Entanglement_and_ClassicalCorrelation.pdf`

- **What:** Analytic proof that classically correlated beams from splitting incoherent thermal light reproduce all qualitative features of entangled 'ghost' imaging, treating PDC and thermal cases in exact parallel.
- **Method:** The authors split a thermal (Gaussian-statistics) beam on a beam splitter into two arms; arm 1 carries the object T(x) plus a bucket/point detector, arm 2 is spatially resolved. Object information lives entirely in the intensity-fluctuation correlation G(x1,x2)=<I1 I2>-<I1><I2>. Using the Gaussian moment-factorization (Siegert relation), they derive G = |tr|^2 |integral h1* h2 <a-dagger(x1)a(x2)>|^2, which is structurally identical to the entangled-case result G=|integral h1 h2 <b1 b2>|^2. Two lens configurations (z=f gives ghost diffraction, z=2f gives ghost image) are analyzed; the second-order coherence function <a-dagger a> plays the role that the entangled pair-correlation U1V2 plays in PDC.
- **Results:** Thermal G scales as <n>^2 vs entangled G ~ <n>+<n>^2; the two differ only in the single-photon regime, where entanglement gives much better visibility (V ~ 0.05 numerically for both in high-flux regime). Numerical double-slit diffraction reconstruction (10000 shots) matches analytic Eq.(14). Classical intensity correlation C = 1 - <N1>/<dN1^2> can approach but never reach the quantum bound; the only genuine quantum advantage is information visibility, not imaging capability.
- **Relevance:** P2/P4 + Separation + Discussion. Directly supports our STANCE (P4): the paper cleanly SEPARATES a physical resource (entanglement) from the imaging outcome, showing the outcome is achievable classically — an analogue of our separation of image QUALITY from measurement ACCOUNTABILITY. Background/must-cite for the GI forward model (bucket + spatially-resolved correlation = our A x = y). Also grounds the coherence-length -> resolution argument relevant to our range-space mode structure.
- **Cite:** Gatti et al. proved analytically that classically correlated thermal beams reproduce all imaging features of entangled ghost imaging, isolating quantum entanglement's sole benefit as information visibility rather than reconstruction ability.
- **💡 Spark:** Their explicit parallel derivation (same G-functional, different physical source) is a template for our Separation section: we can present the certificate contraction lambda/(lambda+sigma_i^2) as source-agnostic, holding across BP/Tikhonov/CS-TV/learned reconstructors just as G holds across thermal/entangled sources. ## 8. Experimental evidence of high-resolution ghost imaging and ghost diffraction with classical thermal light

*arXiv:quant-ph/0408021 (2004); Magatti, Ferri, Gatti, Bache, Brambilla, Lugiato* — `R009_High_Resolution_Ghost_Image_and_Ghost_Diffraction_Experiments_with_Thermal_Light.pdf`

- **What:** Experimental demonstration that a single classical thermal-speckle source split by a beam splitter yields BOTH high-resolution ghost image and ghost diffraction, by changing only the reference arm and leaving the object arm untouched.
- **Method:** A rotating ground glass + turbid latex-sphere cell produces pseudothermal speckle (He-Ne, tau_coh~1s, speckle ~25 um); a beam splitter makes two correlated speckle beams recorded on one CCD. The object (160 um needle in a 690 um aperture) sits in the fixed object arm behind lens F with a bucket detector. Inserting an extra lens F' in the reference arm places the CCD at the conjugate (near-field) plane -> ghost image; removing F' places it at the focal (far-field) plane -> ghost diffraction. Theory follows G(x1,x2)=|tr|^2 |integral h1* h2 Gamma(x,x')|^2 with Gamma the second-order speckle correlation; near-field coherence length dx_n sets image resolution, far-field dx_f sets diffraction resolution.
- **Results:** Measured near-field coherence length dx_n~34.3 um and far-field dx_f~15.6 um (Gaussian fits sigma_n=14.3um, sigma_f=7.8um); the resolution product dx_n * dq = 0.066, far below the unity EPR-like bound previously claimed achievable only with entangled photons, and >=4x better than reported entangled results. Explicitly shows no EPR bound is violated (conditional probability P(x2|x1) has a broad background plus a narrow peak), so near- and far-field speckle sizes are independent, unbounded quantities.
- **Relevance:** P1 (Converse) + Verifiability-Barrier(HINGE) + Governed-Dial. The 'change only the reference arm, leave the object arm untouched' operation is a physical illustration of our governance idea: the measurement of the object is fixed while the RECONSTRUCTION machinery (reference optics) is varied — analogous to injecting null-space content without changing A x = y. Must-cite as experimental GI. The debunking of an over-claimed 'quantum-only' resolution bound parallels our P4 caution against attributing invented capability to a physical/measurement resource.
- **Cite:** Magatti et al. experimentally obtained both ghost image and ghost diffraction from one classical thermal source by altering only the reference arm, and beat a resolution bound thought exclusive to entangled photons (dx_n*dq=0.066 << 1).
- **💡 Spark:** The clean 'object arm fixed, processing arm swappable' structure is a real-optics metaphor for our governed dial: measurement accountability stays invariant while the presentation (image vs diffraction, here; VQAE/VQGAN null content, in ours) changes. Consider citing in the HINGE section to argue that varying downstream presentation cannot add measurement-certified information — the object arm never moved.

## 9. Computational Ghost Imaging (J. H. Shapiro)

*arXiv:0807.2614 (2008); Phys. Rev. A 78, 061802(R)* — `R010_Computational_ghost_imaging.pdf`

- **What:** Shows ghost imaging needs only ONE bucket detector: the high-resolution reference intensity pattern is COMPUTED from the known SLM modulation, proving GI is classical coherence propagation, not nonlocal two-photon interference.
- **Method:** Starting from semiclassical (shot-noise) photodetection of pseudothermal light, Shapiro derives the cross-correlation C(rho1) = background C0 + ghost term C1(rho1) ~ integral exp(-|rho1-rho|^2/rho_L^2)|T(rho)|^2 via the Gaussian moment-factoring theorem, with resolution ~lambda0 L/a0. He then replaces the rotating ground glass with a deterministically driven SLM: because the field E2(rho,t) reaching the object is computable, the intensity pattern I1(rho1,t) that WOULD have hit the reference pinhole is precomputed by diffraction theory, and its mean-subtracted version DeltaI1-tilde is correlated with the single bucket photocurrent to form a background-free ghost image (Eq. 16). Only the object-arm bucket detector physically exists.
- **Results:** Yields background-free imagery in the narrowband limit (DC-block removes the C0 term), controllable resolution lambda0 L/D and field of view lambda0 L/d via SLM pixel geometry, and 3D sectioning (focal depth |DeltaL| <= k0 rho_L^2) by re-correlating the same bucket data against precomputed patterns at many ranges. Contrast ~1/N for binary objects. Because one beam and one detector are used, the image provably cannot arise from nonlocal two-photon interference — establishing GI's classical nature.
- **Relevance:** P1/P3 + Geometry + Governed-Dial + Related-Work. This is the DIRECT ancestor of the single-pixel/computational forward model A x = y that our whole paper is built on: the correlation C(rho1) = sum_m phi_m(rho1) y[m] with known patterns phi_m IS our measurement operator with the reference side computed rather than measured. Must-cite as the computational GI foundation. The precomputed-reference idea (structure lives in the known modulation, not in a second physical beam) directly parallels our certificate being a test-time, ground-truth-free audit of the known operator.
- **Cite:** Shapiro's computational ghost imaging reconstructs an object from a single bucket detector by correlating its photocurrent with the computationally known illumination patterns, proving ghost imaging is classical coherence propagation.

## 10. Single-Pixel Imaging via Compressive Sampling (Duarte, Davenport, Takhar, Laska, Sun, Kelly, Baraniuk)

*IEEE Signal Processing Magazine 25(2):83-91, March 2008* — `R011_Single_pixel_imaging_via_compressive_sampling.pdf`

- **What:** The single-pixel camera: a DMD applies random binary patterns and one photodiode measures y=Phi x = Phi Psi alpha, so a compressible scene is recovered from M<<N sub-Nyquist measurements via l1/TV minimization.
- **Method:** A DMD spatial light modulator sets pixel-wise +-1 (0/1) test functions phi_m; a single photodetector integrates the inner product y[m]=<x,phi_m>, giving the underdetermined linear system y=Phi x=Phi Psi alpha with M<N. Since M<N there are infinitely many x' with Phi x'=y; CS theory says a random incoherent Phi lets sparse/compressible alpha be recovered by l1 basis pursuit (alpha-hat=argmin||alpha||_1 s.t. Phi Psi alpha=y) or, with noise, BPIC (||y-Phi Psi alpha||_2<epsilon), with M>=O(K log(N/K)). Reconstruction uses random Bernoulli/Walsh-Hadamard/noiselet rows and Total Variation minimization. The paper also analyzes multiplexing tradeoffs (dynamic range, quantization, Poisson noise) vs pixel-array/raster/basis scan, and the 'smashed filter' doing classification directly in R^M without reconstruction.
- **Results:** Reconstructed a 256x256 'R' from M=N/50 (50x sub-Nyquist) and a color Mandrill from M=N/10 under low light with a photomultiplier; CS MSE depends only logarithmically on N (M=O(K log(N/K))) vs linear for raster/basis scan; error bound ||alpha-hat - alpha||_2 < C_N epsilon + C_K sigma_K(x); smashed-filter classification uses manifold structure preserved under random projection (M=O(K log N)).
- **Relevance:** P1/P2 + Geometry + Certificate + Separation. This is the canonical undersampled linear operator A (m<<n) with A x = y that is the literal starting point of our paper; the sentence 'since M<N, given y there are infinitely many x' such that Phi x'=y' is EXACTLY our P1 converse premise (feasible-wrong images satisfying the same measurement). Must-cite as the single-pixel/CS forward model and as the sparsity-prior reconstructor (CS-TV) that our certificate audits alongside BP/Tikhonov/learned. ## 43. Neural Discrete Representation Learning (VQ-VAE)

*NeurIPS 2017 (van den Oord, Vinyals, Kavukcuoglu)* — `R054_Neural_Discrete_Representation_Learning.pdf`

- **What:** Introduces the VQ-VAE: an autoencoder whose encoder output is snapped to the nearest entry of a learned discrete codebook (vector quantization), trained with a straight-through estimator plus codebook + commitment losses, giving compact discrete latents that avoid VAE posterior collapse.

## 51. The Perception-Distortion Tradeoff

*CVPR 2018 (extended IEEE T-PAMI 2020 version); Blau & Michaeli* — `R061_The_Perception_Distortion_Tradeoff.pdf`

- **What:** Proves a fundamental, distortion-measure-agnostic tradeoff: lower distortion forces the reconstruction distribution to deviate more from natural-image statistics (worse perceptual quality), so 'consistency-with-reference' and 'looks-real' are formally at odds.
- **Method:** Defines distortion as E[Delta(X,Xhat)] for any full-reference measure and perceptual quality as a divergence d(pX, p_Xhat) (linked to optimal real-vs-fake discrimination: p_success = 0.5*dTV(pX,p_Xhat)+0.5). Introduces the perception-distortion function P(D)=min d(pX,p_Xhat) s.t. E[Delta]<=D. Theorem 2: P(D) is monotonically non-increasing and convex for any convex divergence and ANY distortion measure. Theorem 1: for non-invertible degradations no distortion metric is 'stably distribution preserving.' Theorem 3: for squared error, perfect perceptual quality costs at most Dmax<=2*Dmin (i.e. <=3 dB PSNR), attained by sampling xhat from the posterior p_{X|Y}. Shows GANs with loss = distortion + lambda*adversarial traverse the bound as lambda (a Lagrange multiplier) varies.
- **Results:** Tradeoff is convex and steepest at low distortion / high perceptual quality. Perfect perceptual quality costs at most a factor-2 MSE increase (3 dB PSNR). Empirically SR methods with lowest perceptual index have RMSE ~sqrt(2)x larger than lowest-RMSE methods. 16 SR algorithms plotted on the perception-distortion plane over RMSE/SSIM/MS-SSIM/IFC/VIF/VGG show a blank (unattainable) lower-left corner for every distortion metric; FR and NR metrics anti-correlate near the bound.
- **Relevance:** P1 (Converse) + P3 (Governed Dial) + Related-Work/Discussion. This is the canonical prior result we position against: they show a *statistical/distributional* tradeoff (distribution match vs distortion), whereas our converse is a stronger *geometric/exact* impossibility (feasible-wrong images matching y to ~2e-15 => null content is unverifiable regardless of any statistics). Cite in Verifiability-Barrier (HINGE) and Two-Ledgers as the closest precedent while sharpening our distinction; their lambda-as-Lagrange GAN dial is the conceptual ancestor of our scalar-B dial, which we improve by making it null-space-confined (measurement stays EXACT for every B, not merely a soft tradeoff point).
- **Cite:** Blau & Michaeli prove that for any full-reference distortion measure and non-invertible degradation, decreasing distortion necessarily increases the divergence of the reconstruction distribution from natural-image statistics, a monotone convex perception-distortion bound that no algorithm can cross.
- **💡 Spark:** Their tradeoff is soft and probabilistic (you pay distortion to move along P(D)); OUR dial is qualitatively different because null-space injection keeps A*x_hat_B = y EXACT for every B, so we can dial perceptual content WITHOUT moving along their distortion axis in the measured subspace at all. ## 52. Rethinking Lossy Compression: The Rate-Distortion-Perception Tradeoff

*ICML 2019; Blau & Michaeli* — `R062_Rethinking_Lossy_Compression_The_Rate_Distortion_Perception_Tradeoff.pdf`

- **What:** Extends rate-distortion theory to a triple rate-distortion-perception tradeoff R(D,P): enforcing high perceptual quality provably elevates the rate-distortion curve, so good perception must cost extra rate and/or distortion.

This yields a single gradient step -zeta_i grad_x ||y - A(x_hat_0)||^2 added to each reverse-diffusion update (Gaussian) or a Poisson variant. A Jensen-gap theorem bounds the approximation error. MCG (R074) is recovered as the noiseless special case; unlike projection methods, DPS uses a soft gradient (no hard measurement-consistency projection), giving better paths under noise.
- **Results:** Handles Gaussian and Poisson noise and nonlinear operators (Fourier phase retrieval, nonuniform deblurring) via autodiff; SVD-free. Outperforms projection/spectral baselines (score-SDE, DDRM, MCG) on inpainting, super-resolution, deblurring, phase retrieval on FFHQ/ImageNet. No exact numeric table read here beyond qualitative superiority claims.
- **Relevance:** P2 (Certificate) + P4 (Stance), Related-Work + Separation sections. A canonical learned/generative reconstructor to place in our certificate's cross-reconstructor comparison and to position against: DPS enforces measurement consistency only softly (gradient, not projection), so its outputs generally do NOT satisfy A x_hat = y exactly and inject unconstrained null-space content -- the precise failure mode our P1 converse (consistency != correctness) and P3 governed dial (A x_hat_B = y exactly for all B) are designed to expose and remedy.
