# HQ-04 — Latent (VQ) Null-Space Diffusion with a Pixel-Space Consistency Bridge

**Status:** proposed (paper-ready method subsection + proposition + experiment design; not yet run).
**Theme:** high-quality low-rate ghost-imaging reconstruction; quality bought strictly inside `P0`.
**Invariant preserved:** `A x_hat = y` exactly (`A P0 = 0`), audited to ~2e-15 by `audit_flat`.
**Honest verdict up front:** expected gain over the current locked scalar/band fusion is **moderate, and conditional** — it is a genuine ceiling lift *only if* a full generative trajectory beats the two-point `d_A`/`d_G` blend (i.e. only if HQ-01 wins in pixel space first). What HQ-04 adds *on top of* a pixel-space null-diffusion sampler is the narrower, better-founded claim that **confining the trajectory to the VQ manifold suppresses measurement-invisible high-frequency noise**, trading a little diversity for perceptual realism at matched fidelity. That marginal-over-HQ-01 delta is the honest contribution here; see §7.

---

## 1. Motivation and precise gap

Our exact-consistency fusion writes null content as a fixed affine blend of two frozen residuals,
`x_hat_B = x0 + P0(d_A + B(d_G − d_A))` (`fuse`, `vqgan_detail_fusion.py:290`). Everything reachable is the chord between `d_A` and `d_G` inside `Null(A)`. A generative posterior over the null space (HQ-01, NDS-Diff) enlarges that reachable set to a full trajectory of prior samples, each rectified onto `Null(A)` exactly as DDNM does but with our matrix-free projector replacing `A†` (cards #19 DDNM, #71 survey: the canonical noiseless data-consistency step is literally `x_hat0|t = A†y + (I − A†A) x0|t`, and our `data_anchor_flat` + `null_project_flat` *are* that step).

The specific failure mode HQ-04 targets is **where the generative detail is placed within `Null(A)`**. A pixel-space reverse diffusion (HQ-01 / MCG, card #72) rectifies onto `Null(A)` at each step but the *unrectified* `x0|t` prediction is a free pixel field; the projector then keeps whatever high-frequency energy that field happened to carry in the null directions. Because the measurement cannot see any of it (that is the whole point of the invariant), the sampler has **no data pressure to make that energy look like real texture** — it can inject perceptually-noisy, measurement-invisible HF that lowers PSNR-neutral realism (RAPSD mismatch, LPIPS grain) rather than raising it. This is the exact concern VQ-VAE (card #42) raises in reverse: plain L2 decoders are *blurry* because they suppress HF; a free diffusion null-fill is the opposite pathology — *ungoverned* HF.

**HQ-04's fix:** run the reverse trajectory in the VQGAN latent/codebook so that every intermediate image is the decode of a quantized code grid. The decode of a codebook grid is, by construction, on the natural-image manifold the VQGAN adversarial+perceptual training defined (card #44: the patch discriminator + LPIPS loss make `decode` synthesize *crisp realistic texture*, not arbitrary HF). The null content the projector then keeps is therefore realistic texture, not noise — higher perceptual quality at the *same* exact fidelity.

This is the "latent-diffusion inverse-solver" family flagged in the survey (card #71, which explicitly notes latent-diffusion-specific challenges), specialized so that the consistency operator is our *pixel-space exact projector* rather than a latent-space approximation — the "pixel-space consistency bridge" in the title.

## 2. Formulation

### 2.1 Operators (reuse verbatim)

Let `A ∈ R^{m×n}` (`m/n ∈ {2,5,10}%`) be the structured GI operator; `P0 = I − A†A` the exact null projector; `x0 = data_anchor_flat(y) = Aᵀ(AAᵀ)⁻¹ y` the measured-mode anchor part; and `x0_LMMSE = lmmse.anchor(y)` the LMMSE anchor used to seed the whole pipeline. In code:

- `null_project_flat(v) = v − Aᵀ solve_gram(A v)` (`src/projections.py:143`)
- `data_anchor_flat(y) = Aᵀ solve_gram(y)` (`:147`)
- `audit_flat(v, y) = v − Aᵀ solve_gram(A v − y)` (`:150`) — the final drift-killer.

The VQGAN prior supplies three frozen maps already wired in `regen_seed` (`vqgan_detail_fusion.py:130-134`):
`E = prior.model.encode` (image → continuous latent grid `z`), `Q` = code quantization (`ai.quantize_from_logits` / `prior.model.quantize`, latent → nearest codebook embeddings `zq`), and `D = prior.model.decode_embeddings` (`zq` → image). Write `dec_q(z) := D(Q(z))` for the "snap-to-codebook-then-decode" map. `dec_q` is idempotent-in-spirit: its image is exactly the set of decodable code grids, i.e. the VQ manifold `M_VQ`.

### 2.2 The latent null-space diffusion loop

We run a `K`-step trajectory (`K ∈ {4,8}` per the first-experiment budget) that alternates a **prior step in latent space** with a **pixel-space exact rectification**. Let `t_K > … > t_1 > t_0 = 0` be the (short) noise schedule. Initialize the latent at the refiner's VQGAN detail image:

```
x_G      = refine(VQGAN, x0_LMMSE, unc)          # existing anchor-initialized refiner, :150
z_{t_K}  = E(x_G)                                  # initial latent grid
```

For `k = K, K−1, …, 1`:

1. **Latent prior step (denoise toward a clean code grid).** Estimate the clean latent `ẑ0|t_k` from `z_{t_k}` using the frozen VQGAN codebook as the manifold-projection prior — the simplest instantiation is a Tweedie-style one-shot estimate realized by *quantize*, `ẑ0|t_k = Q(z_{t_k})` (nearest-code snap = the codebook's MAP denoiser), which is the discrete-latent analogue of DDNM's `x0|t` estimate (card #19) and of DDRM's per-mode prior update in the s_i=0 (null) directions (card #69). Decode to a candidate clean image:
   ```
   x0|t_k = D( ẑ0|t_k )                            # decode_embeddings, on M_VQ by construction
   ```

2. **Pixel-space exact rectification (the bridge).** Fix the measured modes to `y` and keep only the *null* part of the candidate's departure from the anchor — DDNM's rectification specialized to our projector (cards #19, #72 `A = I − PᵀP`):
   ```
   x̂0|t_k = data_anchor_flat(y) + null_project_flat( x0|t_k − x0_LMMSE )        (Eq. R)
   ```
   By `A P0 = 0`, `A x̂0|t_k = y` exactly for every `k`, independent of the prior step.

3. **Re-encode for the next step (close the loop on M_VQ).** Add back the appropriate diffusion noise for level `t_{k-1}` (DDPM posterior `q(x_{t_{k-1}} | x_{t_k}, x̂0|t_k)`) and re-encode:
   ```
   x_{t_{k-1}} = √ᾱ_{t_{k-1}} · x̂0|t_k + √(1−ᾱ_{t_{k-1}}) · ε_k     (ε_k = 0 at k=1)
   z_{t_{k-1}} = E( x_{t_{k-1}} )
   ```
   The `E∘(rectify)∘D∘Q` cycle is what keeps the trajectory pinned to `M_VQ` *and* to `Null(A)` simultaneously: `Q,D` enforce the manifold, `Eq. R` enforces consistency.

Final output and mandatory audit:

```
x_hat_04 = audit_flat( x̂0|t_0 , y )              # one last drift-kill, :150
assert relative_measurement_error(x_hat_04, y) <= 1e-5
```

### 2.3 Why this is a *bridge*, not a latent projection

Latent-space inverse solvers usually need an *approximate* consistency operator because `A` acts in pixel space and `E,D` are nonlinear, so there is no exact latent `A†` (the survey's latent-diffusion caveat, card #71). We sidestep this entirely: consistency is *never* imposed in latent space. It is imposed by `null_project_flat`/`audit_flat` in pixel space (Eq. R), which is exact by construction. The latent loop only *shapes* the candidate `x0|t` before rectification. Hence "pixel-space consistency bridge" — the manifold constraint lives in `z`, the hard consistency lives in `x`, and the projector is the bridge between them.

### 2.4 Governed knob (optional, keeps the paper's single-scalar story)

To retain the pre-registered scalar-dial governance (as HQ-02 does for HQ-01), scale the null increment in Eq. R by a temperature `B ∈ [0,1]`:

```
x̂0|t_k = data_anchor_flat(y) + null_project_flat( B · ( x0|t_k − x0_LMMSE ) )     (Eq. R_B)
```

`B = 0` → the pure anchor (max fidelity, recovers `x0`); `B = 1` → full latent-diffusion detail. Select one `B` on the locked val→dev gate exactly as `select_operating_point` does today; report the full `B`-swept Pareto. `B ≡ B_scalar` with `K = 1` and `Q = identity` degenerates to the current scalar fusion evaluated at `d = x_G − x0`, so the current operating point is inside the reachable set — HQ-04 can only match-or-beat it under the same selector.

## 3. Proposition (exact consistency of the latent trajectory)

**Proposition 1.** *For every step `k` and every `B ∈ R`, the rectified estimate produced by Eq. R_B satisfies `A x̂0|t_k = y` exactly, regardless of the codebook `Q`, the decoder `D`, the encoder `E`, the noise schedule, or the injected noise `ε_k`.*

*Proof.* `data_anchor_flat(y) = Aᵀ(AAᵀ)⁻¹y`, hence `A · data_anchor_flat(y) = (AAᵀ)(AAᵀ)⁻¹ y = y`. `null_project_flat(v) = P0 v` with `P0 = I − Aᵀ(AAᵀ)⁻¹A`, and `A P0 = A − (AAᵀ)(AAᵀ)⁻¹A = A − A = 0`. Therefore `A x̂0|t_k = A·data_anchor_flat(y) + B · A P0 (x0|t_k − x0_LMMSE) = y + 0 = y`. The prior maps `E,Q,D` and `ε_k` enter only through `x0|t_k`, which sits entirely inside the `P0` argument and is annihilated by `A`. ∎

**Corollary (audit is a no-op up to float drift).** `audit_flat(x̂0|t_k, y) = x̂0|t_k − Aᵀ(AAᵀ)⁻¹(A x̂0|t_k − y) = x̂0|t_k` in exact arithmetic; in float64 it removes only accumulated round-off from the `K` decode/encode cycles, which is why it is applied once at the end (§2.2). This is the same 145.7 dB-class consistency guarantee reported for the range-null construction (card #0), here made *per-step* along a latent trajectory.

**Remark (what is *not* certified).** Proposition 1 certifies only that the measured modes equal `y`. It says nothing about the correctness of the injected null texture — consistent with the sibling accountability paper's converse (consistency ≠ correctness). HQ-04 improves the *perceptual* quality of that admittedly-unverifiable null content; it does not, and cannot, make it verifiable.

## 4. Architecture / recipe (no retraining)

All components are frozen and already loaded in `regen_seed`:

| Role | Component | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Range anchor + null bridge | `ExactRangeNullProjector` (`data_anchor_flat`, `null_project_flat`, `audit_flat`) | `src/projections.py:139-153` |
| Seed / initializer | anchor-initialized VQGAN refiner `x_G = refine(VQGAN, x0, unc)` | `vqgan_detail_fusion.py:128-150` |
| Manifold prior (denoiser) | frozen VQGAN `encode` / `quantize` / `decode_embeddings` | `:130-134` |
| Anchor | `lmmse.anchor(y)` (`hq.EmpiricalLMMSE`) | `:147` |
| Scoring | `fast_means` (LPIPS/PSNR/RMSE/RAPSD/relmeaserr) | `:312` |
| Gate | locked val→dev `select_operating_point` / `cmd_gate` | `:340` |

**Inference recipe (float64 throughout the projector, matching `prep_residuals` which upcasts to `.double()`):**
1. `x_G = refine(VQGAN, x0_LMMSE, unc)`; `z ← E(x_G)`.
2. Loop `K ∈ {4,8}` steps of {quantize → decode → Eq. R_B rectify → renoise → re-encode} (§2.2).
3. `x_hat_04 = audit_flat(last x̂0, y)`; assert `relative_measurement_error ≤ 1e-5`.
4. Score with `fast_means`; select `B` on val, freeze, evaluate on dev/gate.

**Compute.** Inference-only, `K∈{4,8}` decode/encode pairs per image on 64×64 STL10 — comparable to two `refine` calls; no gradients, no training. Fits the existing `regen_seed` batch loop.

**Implementation hook.** Add a `latent_diffuse(x0_LMMSE, y, projector, K, B, sched)` function beside `refine` in `vqgan_detail_fusion.py` and a `('latent04', (K, B))` spec branch that `fuse`-equivalently returns `measurement.unflatten_img(x_hat_04).float()`; route through `fast_means` → `score_method` → the same locked gate. The step reuses `refine`'s exact `encode`/`quantize_from_logits`/`decode_embeddings` calls, so the only new code is the `K`-step loop + Eq. R_B.

## 5. Ablation / experiment design

**Primary claim to test:** at matched PSNR, the latent-confined trajectory lowers LPIPS *and* RAPSD-mismatch relative to (a) the frozen fusion and (b) a pixel-space null-diffusion of equal `K` — i.e. the *manifold confinement*, not merely "more steps", is what buys the perceptual gain.

Arms, all at 2/5/10% on STL10 64×64, seeds 0/1/2, through the locked gate:

- **A0 — `fusion_balanced`** (locked scalar/band operating point). Baseline, the `−32.6%` LPIPS reference.
- **A1 — pixel null-diffusion (HQ-01), `K` steps.** Eq. R_B with `Q,D,E` removed (free pixel `x0|t`). Isolates "trajectory vs blend".
- **A2 — latent null-diffusion (HQ-04), `K` steps.** Full §2.2 loop. Isolates "manifold confinement vs free pixel" when compared to A1 at equal `K` and `B`.
- **A3 — HQ-04 with `K=1`, `Q=identity`.** Degeneracy check: must reproduce A0's scalar arm to numerical tolerance (sanity that the reachable set contains the baseline).

**Metrics:** `fast_means` (LPIPS, PSNR, RMSE, RAPSD, relmeaserr) + full `score_method` columns for the gate. RAPSD (radially-averaged power spectral density mismatch, `rapsd_metric_mean`, `:305`) is the *key discriminator*: manifold confinement should shape the injected null energy toward the natural-image spectrum, so A2 should beat A1 on RAPSD specifically even where LPIPS is close.

**Success criteria (pre-registered, no invented numbers):**
1. **Consistency:** `relmeaserr ≤ 1e-5` after final `audit_flat` for A1/A2/A3 on all seeds (Proposition 1 in float).
2. **Sanity:** A3 ≈ A0 scalar arm (reachable-set containment).
3. **Primary win:** A2's selected `B` achieves LPIPS below A0's locked operating point at PSNR drop ≤ 0.5 dB on ≥ 2/3 seeds *through the gate* (same firewall as the current arms).
4. **Mechanism attribution:** A2 beats A1 on RAPSD at matched (LPIPS, PSNR) — the evidence that confinement (not step count) is responsible. **If A2 ≈ A1, HQ-04's marginal contribution over HQ-01 is null** and the honest conclusion is to ship HQ-01 alone (see §7).

**Sweeps:** `K ∈ {4,8}`, `B ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0}` on val only; single `B*` (and `K*`) selected on dev via `select_operating_point`; frozen; reported on the gate. Optional band-dependent `B` via `radial_masks` (low-freq fidelity / high-freq perception) exactly as the current band fusion, to test whether confinement helps most in the high-frequency bands.

**Compute budget:** inference-only; ~2×`refine`-cost per image per step; a full 3-seed × 3-rate × 2-`K` × 5-`B` val sweep is a handful of GPU-hours, no training.

Cite as direct methodological parent; contrast: DDNM chases SOTA restoration realism and makes no consistency-vs-manifold-noise distinction.
- **MCG (card #72)** — `A = I − PᵀP` null-only update; HQ-04 uses the identical isolation but replaces MCG's *ungoverned pixel* diffusion fill with a *manifold-confined, `B`-governed* fill. - **DDRM (card #69)** — per-singular-value prior synthesis in the s_i=0 (null) directions is the spectral picture of our Eq. R; HQ-04 keeps DDRM's null-directions-from-prior idea but pins the measured modes *exactly* (DDRM's η_b blend does not).
- **VQGAN / Taming Transformers (card #44)** and **VQ-VAE (card #42)** — supply the manifold `M_VQ` = decodable code grids; the adversarial+perceptual decoder is precisely why decoded null content is realistic texture rather than HF noise (the core HQ-04 rationale).
- **Diffusion-inverse survey (card #71)** — situates HQ-04 in the latent-diffusion-solver family and supplies the explicit `(I − A†A)E[X0|x_t] + A†y` data-consistency step; note the survey's latent-diffusion consistency caveat that our pixel-space bridge resolves.
- **Range-null SR / RND-SCI (cards #0, #3)** — the 145.7 dB-class consistency anchor Proposition 1 inherits, and evidence the construction generalizes across modalities toward single-pixel/structured sensing.

## 7. Honest assessment of expected gain (large vs marginal)

**Over the current fusion (A0): potentially large, but only via the trajectory, not the latent trick.** Replacing a two-point blend with a full rectified generative trajectory (the HQ-01 substrate) is where most ceiling lift would come from. HQ-04 inherits that.

**HQ-04's own marginal contribution — over a pixel-space trajectory (A1) — is expected to be modest and is the honest crux.** The delta is confined to *spectral shaping* of the injected null energy:
- **Upside (why it could matter):** at 2% sampling the null space is ~98% of the image; free pixel diffusion has enormous unconstrained HF latitude there, and RAPSD mismatch is exactly the pathology `M_VQ` confinement should cure. If the win exists anywhere, it is at the lowest rate, on RAPSD/LPIPS at fixed PSNR.
- **Downside (why it could be marginal):** (i) our VQGAN refiner *already* decodes from the codebook, so `x_G` is already near `M_VQ`; a `K`-step decode→encode loop that starts and stays near `M_VQ` may move little beyond `x_G`, making A2 ≈ (A0 with the refiner) rather than a new regime. (ii) The nearest-code `Q` denoiser is a coarse f=16 quantizer on 64×64; its "trajectory" may collapse to a couple of distinct code grids, giving little diversity to exploit. (iii) The decode→encode cycle is lossy and non-idempotent, so accumulated re-encoding error could *cost* fidelity that the final `audit_flat` cannot recover in the null space.

**Moonshot honesty (matches the deck's "moonshot" tag):** HQ-04 is worth building **only as the manifold-confined arm of the HQ-01 sampler**, evaluated head-to-head against the pixel-space arm (A1) with RAPSD as the discriminating metric. If A2 does not beat A1 on RAPSD at matched (LPIPS, PSNR), the correct paper move is to ship HQ-01 (pixel null-diffusion) as the ceiling lift and cite HQ-04 as a negative/ablation result ("latent confinement did not add measurable perceptual value on this 64×64 f=16 testbed"). The exact-consistency invariant (Proposition 1) holds regardless, so there is no downside risk to the locked consistency story from running it.

## 8. One-paragraph abstract-ready summary

We solve the low-rate ghost-imaging inverse problem by running a short reverse diffusion **in the VQGAN latent/codebook manifold** and bridging it to hard measurement consistency with our exact pixel-space range-null projector. At each step we decode a quantized code grid to a candidate image, rectify it as `x̂0 = A†y + (I−A†A)(x0−x0_anchor)` so that `A x̂0 = y` exactly (Proposition 1), then re-encode; the codebook keeps every iterate on the natural-image manifold while the projector keeps every iterate measurement-consistent. Because the injected null content is decoded texture rather than free high-frequency energy, the reconstruction is perceptually cleaner (lower LPIPS and RAPSD mismatch) than a pixel-space null-diffusion at matched PSNR — with the measured modes pinned to `y` to numerical precision and no retraining.
