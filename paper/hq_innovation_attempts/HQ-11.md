# HQ-11 — Anchor-conditioned ΠGDM in the null space (LMMSE second-order statistics steer the sampler)

**Status: PROPOSED** (buildable now; depends on the HQ-1 null-confined sampler as substrate. The LMMSE fit, the exact projector, and the frozen VQGAN score/decoder already exist in this repo; the only new object is the covariance-preconditioned guidance step and its low-rank evaluation.)

**Theme.** High-quality low-rate GI reconstruction — raise the perceptual ceiling of the exact null-space fusion past the locked `−32.6% LPIPS` operating point without paying PSNR.

**Invariant held throughout.** Every edit lives inside the exact null projector: `x̂ = data_anchor_flat(y) + null_project_flat(·)`, so `A x̂ = y` to `~2e-15` because `A P₀ = 0` (`src/projections.py`, `audit_flat`). HQ-11 changes only *how the null content is chosen at each reverse-diffusion step* — the range ledger stays pinned to `y` exactly. This point does **not** relax the consistency invariant.

---

## 1. Motivation and the precise gap

The HQ-1 substrate (NDS-Diff) runs a short reverse-diffusion loop and, at each step, rectifies the current clean estimate `x₀|t` back onto the exact null space:

```
x̂₀|t = data_anchor_flat(y) + null_project_flat( x₀|t − x₀_anchor )         # (DDNM specialized to our exact P₀; R026)
```

This is the *noiseless* range-null update: the measured modes are fixed to `A⁺y` and the null modes are copied straight from the diffusion prior with **unit weight**. It is optimal only in the idealized case where the prior's null estimate needs no correction toward the measurements. But the GI operator carries real structure — some null-adjacent directions are strongly predictable from `y`, others are not — and a *flat, isotropic* copy of `x₀|t` into `Null(A)` ignores all of it. DPS/MCG (R071) inject a likewise isotropic measurement gradient `−ζ ∇‖y − A x̂₀‖²`; the survey R073 documents that this whole family enforces consistency by an *unweighted* gradient or projection.

ΠGDM (Pseudo-inverse-Guided Diffusion Models; catalogued as a distinct family in the R073 survey, alongside DPS/DDRM/DDNM) improves on this by treating `p(y | x₀|t)` as a **Gaussian with a non-trivial covariance** and steering the sampler with a *covariance-weighted* pseudo-inverse likelihood rather than an isotropic gradient. Its guidance is

```
∇_{x_t} log p(y | x_t) ≈ ( ∂x̂₀/∂x_t )ᵀ · Aᵀ ( r² A Aᵀ + σ_y² I )⁻¹ ( y − A x̂₀ ),
```

where `r²` is the per-step Gaussian variance of `p(x₀ | x_t)`. The pseudo-inverse `Aᵀ(r²AAᵀ + σ_y²I)⁻¹` is exactly the geometry-aware reweighting our isotropic step throws away.

**Our unfair advantage.** ΠGDM has to *guess* the covariance `r²` from a schedule. We do not: **we already fit an empirical LMMSE** (`EmpiricalLMMSE` in `gan_high_quality_gi.py`) that provides both the posterior mean `μ_LMMSE(y)` (already used as the range anchor `x₀`) and, in closed form, the *posterior covariance* `C_post` of `x | y` under the empirical Gaussian prior. That covariance **is** the object ΠGDM approximates. So we can build a ΠGDM-style, covariance-weighted null-space guidance for our GI operator *almost for free*, and confine it to `Null(A)` so consistency is exact rather than ΠGDM's soft pseudo-inverse pin.

The bet: steering the null fill by the GI operator's *actual* spectral/statistical geometry — trusting the prior more where the LMMSE says the null direction is well-determined, less where it is not — places sharper detail in the right modes than a flat copy, lifting LPIPS/RAPSD at equal PSNR.

## 2. Method

### 2.1 The LMMSE object we actually own (honest accounting)

`EmpiricalLMMSE.fit(x_train, rows, lambda_)` builds a low-rank empirical Gaussian model of `x` from `N` training images. Writing `Z = (X − μ)/√(N−1)` (`z_scaled`, shape `N×n`), `U = Z Rᵀ` (`u`, shape `N×m`), and `S = UᵀU + λI` (`s`, shape `m×m`, inverse cached as `inv_s`), the empirical prior covariance is `C_xx = ZᵀZ` (rank `≤ N`) and the LMMSE posterior for `x | y` is Gaussian with

```
μ_LMMSE(y) = μ + (y − Rμ) S⁻¹ Uᵀ Z                      # EmpiricalLMMSE.predict_flat, verbatim
C_post     = C_xx − C_xx Rᵀ S⁻¹ R C_xx
           = ZᵀZ − (ZᵀU) S⁻¹ (UᵀZ).                      # low-rank, never formed as n×n
```

The stored `posterior_std` is exactly `sqrt(diag(C_post))` (see `fit`: `post_diag = diag_c − einsum(q, inv_s, q)`), surfaced by `uncertainty_map`. **Key honest constraint:** the repo stores only `diag(C_post)`; the *full* `C_post` is not materialized (it would be `n×n`), but it **is** available in the low-rank factored form `C_post = ZᵀZ − (ZᵀU)S⁻¹(UᵀZ)` from the cached `z_scaled`, `u`, `inv_s`. Any honest "Σ_null" must be expressed through these factors (matvecs only), never as a dense `n×n` matrix.

### 2.2 The null-restricted posterior preconditioner Σ_null

Define the projector's null map `P₀ = null_project_flat` and range map `P_R = row_project_flat` (`src/projections.py`). We want a preconditioner acting on the *null component* of the increment, weighting each null direction by how predictable it is under the LMMSE posterior. Two faithful constructions, in decreasing fidelity/cost:

**(a) Exact low-rank null-restricted posterior operator (the principled ΠGDM analogue).**
Restrict the posterior covariance to the null space, `C_post^0 = P₀ C_post P₀`, and use its (regularized) posterior-precision-weighted contraction as the guidance operator:

```
Σ_null(v) = P₀ [ C_post^0 ( C_post^0 + τ² I )⁻¹ v ]            # a null-restricted Wiener/ΠGDM contraction, τ² a per-step scale
```

Because `C_post = ZᵀZ − (ZᵀU)S⁻¹(UᵀZ)` is rank `≤ N` and `P₀v = v − Aᵀ(AAᵀ)⁻¹Av` is matrix-free, `C_post^0 v` costs two `N×n` matmuls plus one projector call; the `(C_post^0 + τ²I)⁻¹` solve is done with a few conjugate-gradient iterations in the `N`-dim coefficient space (Woodbury on the low-rank factors), never `n×n`. This is the closest exact analogue of ΠGDM's `Aᵀ(r²AAᵀ+σ_y²I)⁻¹` — but built from the GI operator's *empirical* second-order statistics rather than a scheduled scalar `r²`.

**(b) Diagonal-std fallback (cheapest, what the repo exposes today).**
Use the per-pixel posterior std already computed:

```
Σ_null(v) = P₀ [ w ⊙ v ],   w = g(posterior_std) ∈ [0,1]^n,
```

with `g` the same 1–99 percentile normalization `uncertainty_map` already applies (small posterior-std pixel ⇒ well-determined ⇒ trust the prior's null estimate more; large ⇒ down-weight). This is a diagonal ΠGDM preconditioner: cheaper, weaker, but zero new machinery beyond a Hadamard product. Honest note: a *diagonal* map cannot capture cross-pixel spectral coupling, so (b) is a lower bound on what (a) can do — its expected gain over the isotropic HQ-1 step is small (§5).

### 2.3 The guided reverse step

At each of `K=20` reverse-diffusion steps, with the frozen VQGAN as the `x₀`-predictor / score (`decode_embeddings` on the refiner path in `regen_seed`, no retraining):

```
1.  x₀|t          = VQGAN_x0_predict(x_t)                          # Tweedie posterior mean (R071), frozen prior
2.  Δ             = μ_LMMSE(y) − x₀|t                              # anchor-conditioned residual toward the LMMSE mean
3.  x₀_guided     = x₀|t + Σ_null( Δ )                             # ΠGDM covariance-weighted pull, restricted to Null(A) via P₀ in Σ_null
4.  x̂₀           = data_anchor_flat(y) + null_project_flat( x₀_guided − x₀_anchor )   # exact range pin + null rectify (DDNM/R026)
5.  x_{t−1}       ~ p( · | x_t, x̂₀ )                              # standard reverse update on the rectified estimate
```

`x₀_anchor := data_anchor_flat(y) = A⁺y` is the fixed range point. Note **step 4 makes the range ledger exact regardless of what Σ_null does in step 3**: any range-space component Σ_null accidentally introduces is discarded by `null_project_flat`, and the measured modes are re-pinned to `A⁺y`. This is precisely where we beat ΠGDM: ΠGDM's pseudo-inverse step only *softly* enforces `Ax̂₀ ≈ y`; our exact `P₀` makes it hold to machine precision. After the final step, one `audit_flat(x̂, y)` kills float drift.

**Formulation collapse checks (sanity anchors).**
- `Σ_null ≡ P₀` (identity contraction, `τ→0` in (a) or `w≡1` in (b)) recovers the **isotropic HQ-1 / DDNM** step exactly ⇒ HQ-11 is a strict generalization and can only match-or-beat its substrate on the dev-selected point.
- `Σ_null ≡ 0` recovers the pure range anchor `x₀ = A⁺y` (max fidelity, no invented detail).
- The steering strength can be exposed as a single governed scalar (a temperature on `Σ_null`, e.g. `Σ_null → γ·Σ_null`) making HQ-11 sit under the HQ-2 "B-as-guidance-scale" governance story: one pre-registered scalar `γ` selected on the locked gate.

## 3. Proposition (why the exact-P₀ variant is well-posed and consistency is free)

**Proposition (exact-consistency covariance-guided null sampling).** *Let `A ∈ ℝ^{m×n}`, `m<n`, `y = A x_gt`, `P₀ = I − A⁺A`, and let `Σ_null` be any operator whose range lies in `Null(A)` (both §2.2 constructions satisfy `A·Σ_null(v) = 0` because they end in `P₀`). Then for every reverse-diffusion step, the rectified estimate `x̂₀ = A⁺y + P₀(x₀_guided − A⁺y)` satisfies `A x̂₀ = y` exactly, independent of `Σ_null`, `τ`, `γ`, and the prior `x₀|t`. Moreover, if `Σ_null(v) = P₀ C_post^0 (C_post^0 + τ²I)⁻¹ v` with `C_post ⪰ 0` (the empirical posterior covariance), then `Σ_null` is a contraction (`‖Σ_null‖ ≤ 1`) and `x₀_guided` is a convex-type interpolation between the prior estimate and the LMMSE-preferred direction, so the step cannot amplify the increment beyond the posterior-supported range.*

**Proof sketch.** `A x̂₀ = A A⁺y + A P₀(·) = y + 0`, since `A A⁺ = I` on `range(A)` and `A P₀ = 0` by construction (verified numerically at `~2e-15` in float64 throughout this repo). Consistency therefore holds for *any* `Σ_null` ending in `P₀` — the covariance guidance is unconstrained by the data-fit requirement, which is exactly the design freedom ΠGDM lacks (its pseudo-inverse must simultaneously fit and steer). For the contraction claim: `C_post ⪰ 0` (posterior covariance of a Gaussian), so `C_post^0 = P₀ C_post P₀ ⪰ 0`, and `C_post^0 (C_post^0 + τ²I)⁻¹` has eigenvalues `ρ_j/(ρ_j+τ²) ∈ [0,1)`; composing with the orthogonal projector `P₀` keeps `‖Σ_null‖ ≤ 1`. ∎

**Connection to GSNR predictability (R003).** The eigenvalues `ρ_j/(ρ_j+τ²)` are precisely the null-mode analogue of GSNR's per-mode predictability `ρ_j² ≤ c_j/(c_j+μ_j)`: directions with large posterior variance `ρ_j` (well-supported by the empirical prior) are trusted; directions the data/prior cannot resolve are shrunk toward zero. HQ-11 thus operationalizes GSNR's "predictability" as a *sampler preconditioner*, not just an offline basis-selection criterion — and it does so from the operator's empirical statistics rather than a hand-chosen graph Laplacian. This is the honest novelty seam vs R003.

**Honesty guard.** The proposition asserts only exact data consistency and boundedness. It makes **no** claim that the covariance-guided null fill is *correct* — the measurement never certifies null content (the paper's standing stance). `Σ_null` chooses a *better-motivated* null point, not a certified one.

## 4. Experiment / ablation design

**Substrate.** Requires the HQ-1 null-confined sampler in place (shared reverse loop + VQGAN `x₀`-predictor). HQ-11 is the drop-in `Σ_null` in step 3.

**Standalone pilot (seed0 val cache, no gate spend).** Script importing `gan_high_quality_gi` (LMMSE) + `src.projections`; on the cached `seed0_val.pt` residual pack, run `K=20`-step samplers and score via `fast_means` (LPIPS, PSNR, RAPSD, RelMeasErr):

| Arm | Σ_null | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| (a) isotropic null-DDNM | `P₀` (identity) | HQ-1 substrate baseline |
| (b0) diagonal ΠGDM | `P₀[w⊙v]`, `w=g(posterior_std)` | cheapest LMMSE preconditioner (§2.2b) |
| (b1) low-rank ΠGDM | `P₀ C_post^0 (C_post^0+τ²I)⁻¹ v` | principled preconditioner (§2.2a) |
| (c) fusion_balanced | — | locked `−32.6%` reference point |

**Primary success criterion.** (b1) lowers LPIPS **and** RAPSD-mismatch vs (a) at equal-or-better PSNR (ΔPSNR ≥ −0.1 dB), with `RelMeasErr ≤ 1e-5` after `audit_flat` on all arms. Secondary: (b1) > (b0) confirms the covariance (not just the diagonal) is carrying the gain — this is the pivotal ablation, because if (b0) ≈ (b1) the effect is diagonal-only and marginal.

**Sweeps.** `τ² ∈ {log-grid over posterior-eigenvalue scale}` and the governed scalar `γ ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0}` on val; select **one** `(τ², γ)` on the locked dev/gate split (`select_operating_point`), then report the full `γ`-swept perception–distortion curve and confirm on seeds 1–2 (multi-seed firewall).

**Rate coverage.** 2/5/10% structured operators (DCT/Hadamard/random rows) — HQ-11 is expected to help most at 2–5% where the null space is largest and the isotropic copy is weakest.

**Cost.** Inference-only. Added cost per step over HQ-1: one `μ_LMMSE(y)` matvec (already computed for the anchor, reusable) + one `Σ_null` evaluation (two `N×n` matmuls + a few CG iters for (b1); a Hadamard product for (b0)). Negligible vs the VQGAN forward pass.

**Kill criteria (pre-registered, honest).** Declare HQ-11 a non-win and fall back to HQ-1 if either: (i) (b1) does not beat (a) on LPIPS at ΔPSNR ≥ −0.1 dB on ≥2/3 seeds, or (ii) (b1) ≈ (b0) within noise (covariance adds nothing beyond the diagonal). Both outcomes are reportable negatives that sharpen the paper's "isotropic null-fill is already near-optimal for this operator" statement.

## 5. Honest assessment of expected gain

**Marginal-to-moderate, and gated on HQ-1 winning first.** This is not a top-3 ceiling bet, for three compounding reasons:

1. **It is a *refinement* of HQ-1's substrate, not new detail.** Like HQ-2, HQ-11 has no value on its own: if the underlying null-confined *sampler* (HQ-1) does not already beat the two-point fusion, a better preconditioner on its steps cannot manufacture a win. The whole gain is "steer the same generative posterior better," not "add a richer posterior."

2. **The exact-P₀ move already absorbs most of what ΠGDM buys.** ΠGDM's headline advantage over DPS/DDNM is *soft, covariance-aware* data consistency under noise. But our operating regime is **noiseless `y`** and we enforce consistency **exactly** via `P₀` (step 4). In the noiseless-exact-projection setting, the pseudo-inverse term ΠGDM adds is largely subsumed by the hard projection — so the *distinctive* contribution here shrinks to "reweight the null direction by posterior predictability," a second-order effect. (ΠGDM's full strength would show under the noisy-`y` regime, which is HQ-14's territory, not this arm's.)

3. **The cheap variant is diagonal-only.** The (b0) fallback the repo exposes today is a per-pixel Hadamard weight; a diagonal cannot capture the cross-mode spectral coupling that is the real content of `C_post`. The honest expected gain of (b0) over isotropic is *small*. The (b1) low-rank variant is where any real lift lives, and its magnitude is genuinely uncertain until run — the pivotal (b1)-vs-(b0) ablation exists precisely to measure it.

**Where it could still matter.** At the lowest rate (2%), the null space dominates and an isotropic copy is at its weakest; if any regime rewards geometry-aware steering, it is there. And the *narrative* value is real independent of the delta: HQ-11 lets the paper say it uses the GI operator's **own fitted second-order statistics** to steer a diffusion sampler with **exact** consistency — a clean, defensible ΠGDM-vs-DDNM positioning (R073/R071/R026) and a concrete operationalization of GSNR predictability (R003) as a sampler preconditioner rather than an offline basis choice. That is a differentiation-and-completeness contribution more than a ceiling-raising one.

**Recommendation.** Build it only *after* HQ-1 is confirmed to win, as a low-cost "does the operator's covariance help steer the sampler?" ablation with a pre-registered kill criterion. Expected LPIPS delta over a working HQ-1: small but possibly non-trivial at 2%; over the current fusion: entirely contingent on HQ-1 first clearing the bar.

## 6. Grounding (cards)

- **ΠGDM family / range-null data-consistency taxonomy** — Daras et al., *A Survey on Diffusion Models for Inverse Problems*, arXiv 2024 (**R073**): places ΠGDM as a distinct covariance-aware likelihood-approximation family and states the canonical `(I − A⁺A)E[X₀|x_t] + A⁺y` noiseless update we specialize.
- **Tweedie posterior mean `x̂₀ = E[x₀|x_t]` as the `x₀`-predictor; soft-gradient guidance we improve on** — Chung et al., *Diffusion Posterior Sampling (DPS)*, ICLR 2023 (**R071**).
- **Exact range-null rectification (`A⁺y + (I−A⁺A)x̄`, `A x̂ = y` exactly)** — Wang, Yu & Zhang, *DDNM/DDNM+*, ICLR 2023 (**R026**); our step 4, but with the matrix-free `ExactRangeNullProjector` instead of a task-specific `A⁺`, and DDNM+'s `Σ_t`-scaled range correction contrasted against our exact pin.
- **Transform-basis measured/null split with a `λ`-weighted proximal knob** — Song et al., *Solving Inverse Problems in Medical Imaging with Score-Based Generative Models*, ICLR 2022 (**R069**): the proximal weight `λ` is the soft cousin of our exact `P₀`; motivates the `γ`/`τ²` steering scalar.
- **Per-mode predictability `ρ_j² ≤ c_j/(c_j+μ_j)` under a Gaussian prior; null-mode coverage** — Gualdrón-Hurtado et al., *GSNR*, arXiv 2026 (**R003**): our `Σ_null` eigen-shrinkage `ρ_j/(ρ_j+τ²)` is the sampler-preconditioner instantiation of GSNR predictability; positioned against their offline graph-Laplacian basis choice.
- **Assets:** `EmpiricalLMMSE` (`gan_high_quality_gi.py`, `predict_flat`/`posterior_std`/low-rank `z_scaled·u·inv_s` factors); `ExactRangeNullProjector` (`src/projections.py`); frozen VQGAN refiner/decoder and locked val→dev gate (`vqgan_detail_fusion.py`).

*Nothing above is claimed as a result. The LMMSE fit, the low-rank posterior factors, the exact projector, the frozen VQGAN score, and the locked gate already exist in this repo; the only new objects are `Σ_null` (low-rank and diagonal variants) and the covariance-guided reverse step, which sit on top of the HQ-1 sampler. Invariant intact: all quality is bought inside `P₀` (`A x̂ = y` to `~2e-15`), and no null content is claimed to be certified by the measurement.*
